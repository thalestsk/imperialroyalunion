The Citizenship and Residency Commission is a Government Commission of HIM Government, responsible for the research and review of citizenship and permanent residency policy, specifically on matters of dual citizenship and deportation of asylum seekers through the Walmarck Asylum Protocol. It is headed by the Commissioner for Citizenship & Residency, who is assisted by Assistant Commissioners, as well as Adjunct Commissioners from the field of immigration law.

About Us

Dual Citizenship Division

The Dual Citizenship Division is responsible for the research and formulation of the nation's dual citizenship policy and the rights, duties, privileges, and obligations of citizens. Enforcement of the policy is relegated to the Citizenship and Naturalisation Agency.

Residency Division

The Residency Division is responsible for the research and formulation of the nation's permanent and temporary residency policy and the rights, duties, privileges, and obligations such residents. Enforcement of the policy is relegated to the National Migration Agency and the Border Protection Service.

Asylum Division

The Asylum Division is responsible for the administration and oversight of the Walmarck Asylum Protocol as executed by the Border Protection Service for the deportation of illegal immigrants and asylum seekers. From time to time, specific appeals against the deportation of specific persons which merit consideration are examined by the Division and escalated to the Commissioners for deliberation.
