State Honours and Heraldry Authority

Autorité héraldique de l'État | Statens heraldiska myndighet

The State Honours and Heraldry Authority is a Statutory Department of HIM Government responsible for the issuance, use, and display of honours and heraldic emblems of Imperial Royal Union. Such heraldic emblems include the State Flag, the Coat of Arms of Imperial Royal Union and the Thalean Royal Family, other territorial emblems and insignia, and personal coat of arms of the descendants of nobility. The use of courtesy titles and honours are also managed and regulated by the Authority. The recognition and display of foreign-issued honours within Imperial Royal Union for persons who are not foreign dignitaries is subject to the approval of the Authority.

The titles of the rank of "Marquis/Marquise" and above are Princely Titles and are used exclusively for the Royal Family but are not representative of lands attached to the title, whereas the remaining titles are Noble Titles and associated with lands. The lands associated to a Baron/Baroness are purely ceremonial, while the lands associated to a Count/Countess or Viscount/Viscountess are located within the Crown Domain and therefore provide a revenue. The revenue of a county is split equally between the Crown and the Count/Countess, compared to a 75%-25% split in favour of the Crown for a viscountcy.

All titles are non-hereditary unless otherwise permitted by the Emperor. The spouse of a holder of a Noble Title is entitled to use the same title and style, while the children may only use the title but not the style of the rank immediately below the holder's rank.
