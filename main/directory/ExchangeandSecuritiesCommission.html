The Exchange and Securities Commission is a Government Commission of HIM Government, responsible for the regulation of market oversight policy pertaining to exchange markets and tradeable securities. The mission of the Commission is to ensure the stability of financial markets through the regulation of capital markets and the enforcement of securities laws. It is headed by the Commissioner for Exchange and Securities, who is assisted by Assistant Commissioners.

About Us

Three Exchanges System

Imperial Royal Union's exchange eco-system comprises of 3 main exchange operators - the Port Imperial Exchange (PIPEX), the Sydonis Stock Exchange (SYDSE), and the Beruma Mercantile Exchange (BERMEX). Located in the 3 timezones across the nation, the PIPEX opens earlier from 8.00 AM to 4.00 PM Western Imperialean Time (UTC+8); the SYDSE opens from 9.00 AM to 5.00 PM Central Imperialean Time (UTC+9); and the BERMEX opens from 10.00 AM to 6.00 PM Eastern Imperialean Time (UTC+10). Together the national financial markets are open for an extended duration of 12 trading hours.

In addition, both the SYDSE and BERMEX are subject to further regulations by their respective Cantonal Governments in which they are domicile. This means that there are 3 unique sets of trading rules which affect the liquidity, volatility, spread, efficiency, and the overall stability of the markets. As a result of this arrangement, there are significant arbitrage opportunities between the exchange markets.

The PIPEX, the SYDSE, and the BERMEX are operated independently of each other by different companies, although the common shareholder is HIM Government who owns 25% of each exchange through the sovereign wealth fund Albatross Group Holding's subsidiary, Albatross Financial Holdings. Institutional investors and private accredited investors are prohibited from holding more than 1% in shares of more than 1 exchange, unless the holdings are held in exchange-traded funds.
