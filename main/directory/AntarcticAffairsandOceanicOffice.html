Antarctic Affairs and Oceanic Office

Bureau antarctique et océanique | Antarktiska byrån

The Antarctic Affairs and Oceanic Office ("A2O2") is an Executive Support Office of HIM Government responsible for the nation's Antarctic policy, primarily the nation's rights to access, exploration, expansion, and development within the Antarctic. A2O2 supports maritime traffic in the Antarctic by providing weather reports and hydrographic surveys, and in recent years has had its remit expanded to include the real-time monitoring and surveillance of icebergs due to melting glacial sheets as a result of global warming. Natural resources management in the Antarctic remains with the relevant public bodies, although A2O2 retains oversight for access to such natural resources.

About Us

Policy Division

The Policy Division is responsible for the research, formulation, and revision of Antarctic policies.

Surveillance Division

The Surveillance Division is responsible for monitoring the hydrological and geological conditions of the Antarctic and its surrounding seas and the real-time monitoring of the movement of glacial ice and floating icebergs.

Projects Division

The Projects Division is responsible for pursuing exploratory and research missions of the Antarctic, including the operation of research stations in the Antarctic. To support its operations, it has a direct line of contact with the Southern Force Projection Command (SUDCOM) of the Imperialean National Armed Forces for civil-military liaison purposes.
