Department of Energy Resources

Département des ressources énergétiques | Energiresursavdelningen

The Department of Energy Resources is the regulatory body for the licensing, conduct, and operation of mining and drilling activities on proven energy resources of Imperial Royal Union. The Department is responsible for ensuring a sustainable supply of energy resources in order to control energy prices in the nation, and in doing so is also responsible for the stockpiling of energy reserves. The Department also engages in arbitrage on the global commodity markets as part of this stockpiling policy. Nuclear fuel and the mining of uranium falls outside of the purview of the Department.

Further Information

Energy Partnership

Energy in Imperial Royal Union is based on a quadruple partnership pact - the Department of Energy Resources regulates the resources producing the energy; the Renewable Energy Commission plans for solar, wind and hydroelectric energy capacities; the Public Utilities Commissions regulates the national grid and monitors the price action of energy; and the Cantonal Governments provide for or contract out the services for power generation that feed into the national grid.

Energy Prices

Energy prices in Imperial Royal Union are kept relatively stable (not getting cheaper or more expensive) not due to intervention from the Department but because of a progressive shift towards renewable energy. Imperial Royal Union's coal and oil reserves are not expected to last for more than 25 years, and the mining and drilling of such fossil fuels has been halted (as part of the stockpiling plan). The entire Territory of Southern Isles is not only a rich fishing ground but also a sizeable natural gas basin. Exploration works are incomplete due to the extreme depths of the seas around the basin. Despite this, energy production from fossil fuels account for less than 20% of national energy production, of which all of it is either imported from the international grid or from imported fossil fuels.
