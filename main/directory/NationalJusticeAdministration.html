The National Justice Administration is the legal arm of HIM Government. It is responsible for the prosecution of all criminal offences across the nation in the courts and for the provision of legal advice for the government on matters of international, civil and constitutional law. While other departments under HIM Government may have their own specialised legal team, the Administration renders assistance in setting up such legal teams. Territorial Administrative Authorities and Cantonal Governments have their own independent legal counsels to prevent a conflict of interest when HIM Government is required to pursue legal action against the local authorities.

Further Information

Prosecutions Division

The Prosecutions Division is responsible for prosecuting criminal offences in the courts. Upon referral of cases from the Directorate-General of Public Security, cases are only brought forward for prosecution at its discretion. The Prosecutions Division is the largest and has regional offices within every Canton.

Civil Law Division

The Civil Law Division is responsible for responding to all legal actions taken against HIM Government, as well as for pursuing legal actions against local authorities. It assists the Attorney-General in advising HIM Government on matters of constitutionality.

International Law Division

The International Law Division is deals primarily on matters of international law and conducts most of its work in international courts and arbitration centres. It is also responsible for leading extradition efforts in overseas courts as well as preventing the extradition of Imperialeans to unfriendly jurisdictions.

Management Office

The Management Office manages the adminstrative, financial and staffing requirements of the Administration. It is in charge of headhunting and hiring of the legal and paralegal staff.

Legislation Office

The Legislation Office assists HIM Government in drafting and amending of Acts of Estates-General, subsidiary legislation and written executive instructions or internal circulars.
