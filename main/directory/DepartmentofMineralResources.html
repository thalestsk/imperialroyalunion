Department of Mineral Resources

Département des ressources minérales | Mineraltillgångsavdelningen

The Department of Mineral Resources is the regulatory body for the licensing, conduct, and operation of mining activities on proven mineral resources of Imperial Royal Union. Mineral policy is a shared responsibility between the Department and the Cantonal Governments - the Department is responsible for mineral economics, output quotas and quality, and the price action of commodity markets, while the Cantonal Governments regulate the safety, sizes, and operating processes of the mines within their Cantons. The Department works closely with the Office of the Surveyor-General in determining the size and precise location of mineral deposits.

General Regulations

Any mineral ore mined within Imperial Royal Union must be accompanied with a batch certificate. The batch certificate indicates general details of the mine operator and their relevant mining permit, geological location and formation, as well as the quality and grade of the mineral according to the Standard Mineral Index defined by the Department but assessed by an independent third-party. Mineral ores not accompanied by such a batch certificate are treated as state property and the proceeds of the sale of such mineral ores must be returned to HIM Government.

Proven Mineral Reserves

The Proven Mineral Reserves of Imperial Royal Union are quoted in millions of tonnes and split according to the Territory in which they are located. Mineral deposits in the Overseas Territories are not classified here (current legislation prevents the local governments of the Overseas Territories from tapping into their local mineral deposits and as such are left undeveloped).
