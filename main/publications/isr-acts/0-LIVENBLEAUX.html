ISR Cap. LIVENBLEAUX
In Force

Edict of Livenbleaux (2019)

His Imperial Majesty's Imperial Edict proclaimed hereforth at Livenbleaux in the year 2019 for the establishment of His Imperial Majesty's Government for the peaceful and orderly governance of the realm.

Preamble

His Imperial Majesty Emperor Thales XIII, Emperor of Imperial Royal Union and Grand Prince of the Insular Realms,

In consideration for the protection and furtherance of the prosperity of the realm, having undertaken the necessary due deliberations in upholding the state of peace and order of the State, and in recognition of the necessity of such action to be duly exercised hereafter,

By this perpetual and irrevocable edict that shall apply universally to His Imperial Majesty's subjects of Imperial Royal Union, in succession of that which has been established and proclaimed, hereby establishes and proclaims:

  1. His Imperial Majesty's Government
    1. The collective body of officers forming the executive, legislature and judiciary shall constitute His Imperial Majesty's Government ("HIM Government").
  2. Vesting of Power
    1. Every officer of HIM Government is vested with the power to act on behalf of the Emperor and shall only do so upon having received their letters patent certifying their appointment.
  3. His Imperial Majesty's Privy Council
    1. His Imperial Majesty's Privy Council ("Privy Council") shall comprise of officers from HIM Government, with the composition of members determined at the discretion of the Emperor.
    2. The Privy Council shall be vested with the power to:
      1. assist the Emperor in the governance of the realm;
      2. issue and revoke letters patent;
      3. declare and annull a state of emergency or war;
      4. make and ratify treaties and international agreements;
      5. advise, direct, instruct, manage, order, oversee, review, and supervise the work and officers of HIM Government;
      6. determine the organisation of and structure within HIM Government;
      7. adjudicate on judicial matters brought before the Emperor; and
      8. exercise other powers as may be vested from time to time by the Emperor.
  4. The Estates-General
    1. The Estates-General shall be the bicameral legislature comprising the Senate and the Chamber of Deputies ("Chamber").
    2. Members of the Estates-General shall receive the titles of "Senator" and "Deputy" respectively and shall be appointed at the discretion of the Emperor.
    3. The Senate shall be vested with the power to originate, enact, amend and repeal Acts of Estates.
    4. The Chamber shall be vested with the power to debate and discuss Acts of Estates, bills and petitions brought before the Chamber, and then refer its recommendations and findings to the Senate for further deliberation.
    5. The specific composition, rules, procedures, appointments and leadership of the Estates-General shall be determined by an Act of Estates.
    6. All Acts of Estates, upon enactment, amendment or repeal, shall come into force only after being assented to by the Emperor.
    7. In the absence of the Estates-General, the Privy Council shall exercise the functions and powers of the Estates-General.
  5. Lords of the State
    1. Any member of HIM Government may be made appointed as a Lord of the State ("Lord") at the discretion of the Emperor and shall be entitled to the courtesy title of "Lord" or "Lady".
    2. Lords may not be brought before a court of law or a hearing, except before the Emperor, for the period of time in which they were a Lord and for the actions and decisions undertaken when they were a Lord, regardless of whether they were acting in their capacity as a Lord.
  6. The Supreme Court of Judicature
    1. The leadership of the Supreme Court of Judicature ("Court") shall comprise of at least 7 Chief Justices to be appointed at the discretion of the Emperor.
    2. The Court shall be vested with the power to:
      1. adjudicate on all judicial matters brought before the Court except for those adjudicated by the Emperor;
      2. interpret all laws within Imperial Royal Union;
      3. review and overturn laws that may be in conflict with Edicts and Acts of Estates;
      4. organise the structure of the Court and establish inferior courts as may be expedient for the exercise of the functions and powers of the Court;
      5. nominate judicial officers to be confirmed at the discretion of the Emperor; and
      6. appoint officers to support the work of the Court and its judicial officers.
    3. The Supreme Court of Judicature ("Court") shall be vested with the power to adjudicate on all judicial matters in the realm.
  7. Administrative divisions

      8. Administrative divisions of the first order in which the nation shall be subdivided into shall be the Territories.

      Administrative divisions of the second order in which Territories shall be subdivided into shall be the Cantons.

      Administrative divisions of the third order in which Cantons shall be subdivided into shall be the Prefectures.

      Each Canton shall designate 1 Prefecture as the Metropolitan Area.

      The order of precedence shall be such that Territories precede Cantons, which then precede the Metropolitan Areas followed by the Prefectures, and within the administrative divisions of the same order, the order of precedence shall be decided by the Privy Council.

  8. Territorial Administrative Authorities
    1. Each Territory shall be collectively governed by a Chief Administrator, who shall be appointed at the discretion of the Emperor, and shall then establish the Territorial Administrative Authority of the Territory as the symbol of authority of the Emperor in residence of the Territory.
    2. The Chief Administrator shall be vested with the powers to:
      1. form, determine and modify the composition and structure of the Territorial Administrative Authority;
      2. appoint and dismiss officers in the Territorial Administrative Authority;
      3. delegate roles and responsibilities to officers in the Territorial Administrative Authority;
      4. draft and propose Devolution Protocols that shall come into force only after being assented to by the Emperor;
      5. define, delimit and modify the borders and boundaries of its subordinated Cantons;
      6. advise and implement the method of selection of candidates for the position of Governor of a Canton within the Territory when consulted on or instructed by the Emperor;
      7. maintain a civilian contingent force for the provision, supplementation, augmentation and coordination of emergency services and disaster management systems;
      8. organise, incorporate and dissolve bodies corporate for the promotion of economic activity and provision of supplementary public services within the Territory;
      9. build, demolish, maintain and plan transport, telecommunications and utilities infrastructure within the Territory with permission from the Cantons; and
      10. implement other laws and exercise other powers as may be devolved to the Chief Administrator by the Emperor.
  9. Cantonal Government
    1. Each Canton shall be collectively governed by a Governor, who shall be appointed at the discretion of the Emperor as the chief executive and representative of the Emperor in the Canton, and shall then establish the Cantonal Government of the Canton.
    2. In the absence of direction and instruction or legal instrument of any contrary intention from HIM Government, the Governor shall be vested with the powers to:
      1. form, determine and modify the composition and structure of the Cantonal Government;
      2. appoint and dismiss officers in the Cantonal Government;
      3. delegate roles and responsibilities to officers in the Cantonal Government;
      4. originate, enact, amend or repeal Cantonal Statutes that shall only apply within the Canton;
      5. appropriate monies allocated to the Cantonal Government;
      6. plan, organise and maintain uniformed forces for the provision of emergency medical and fire services;
      7. formulate and implement social, economic, infrastructure, environmental, and cultural development as well as public education and public health programmes; and
      8. advise, consult, implement and reform local policies as may from time to time be devolved to the Cantonal Government.
    3. The powers vested in the Governor may be divested from the Governor by a Devolution Protocol in which the powers shall then be vested into other officers or bodies of officers within the Cantonal Government.
  10. Overseas Territories
    1. Overseas territorial possessions of Imperial Royal Union shall be constituted into Residencies and governed by a Resident-General, who shall be appointed at the discretion of the Emperor, and shall then establish the local governments of Residency as the symbol of authority of the Emperor in residence of the overseas territories.
    2. In the absence of direction and instruction or legal instrument of any contrary intention from HIM Government, the Governor shall be vested with the powers to:
      1. form, determine and modify the composition and structure of the local governments of the Residency;
      2. appoint and dismiss officers in the local government of the Residency;
      3. delegate roles and responsibilities to officers in the local government of the Residency;
      4. define, delimit and modify the borders and boundaries of the administrative divisions within the Residency;
      5. originate, enact, amend or repeal Organic Laws that shall only apply within the Residency;
      6. implement other laws and exercise other powers as may be devolved to the Resident-General by the Emperor.

Explanatory Notes

Imperial Edicts ("Edicts") are legal instruments proclaimed by the Emperor and form the basis for the law of the land. Edicts are perpetual for the lifetime of the reigning Emperor and irrevocable - a de facto revocation occurs once a new Edict declaring the contrary intention to a current one is proclaimed.

The very first Edict of Livenbleaux was proclaimed in 2012 at the accession of Emperor Thales XIII to the throne, which effectively ensured the continuity of government by recognising the government of the late Emperor Thales XII as his government (since the Edict establishing the government under Emperor Thales XII automatically expired upon his passing, which meant the then government would legally cease to be the government if not re-established by another Edict).

Under Emperor Thales XIII, the structure of the government underwent 3 major changes in 2014, 2017 and finally in 2019, culminating in the current iteration of His Imperial Majesty's Government. The Edict of Livenbleaux thus superseded the previous Edicts when it was proclaimed.

Historically, the Edicts that establish the governments of the Imperialean monarchs often went through multiple changes in the first decade of their reign, in which they were used to refine the size and structure to the style of governance of the reigning monarch. These Edicts would then survive in its current form for the next few decades, and perhaps with occasional tweaks and balances. The key importance of these Edicts is derived from the declaration of intention by the reigning monarch to rule - 13 out of 18 monarchs used these Edicts to establish absolutist rule, while 5 successive monarchs from (Emperor Thales XI to Emperor Thales XII, inclusive of Empress Rothsanna II) used Edicts to establish constitutional governments with parliamentary systems, while the final authority still rested with them (even though they hardly ever used it, except to proclaim their Edicts).
