ISR Cap. 34
In Force

Migration and Residency Act

  1. Short title and commencement
    1. This Act may be cited as the "Migration and Residency Act".
    2. The following provisions of this Act are effective as of 14 May 2021.
  2. Director of Migration Services
    1. The Director of Migration Services (“Director”) shall be the head of the National Migration Agency and shall be appointed at the discretion of the Emperor.
    2. The powers and discretions vested in the Director under this Act, and the duties required to be discharged by him may be exercised and discharged by subordinate officers within the National Migration Agency duly authorised and appointed, directly or indirectly, by the Director to act on his behalf, in which such an officer shall be referred to as an “authorised officer”.
    3. The Emperor and any representative duly authorised by the Emperor may give directions of a general character in relation to provisions not completely provided for under this Act for the purposes of specifying the scope of responsibilities and duties or for the clarification of ambiguity, as to the exercise of the powers and discretions conferred on the Director by, and the duties required to be discharged by the Director under, this Act or any other written law in force, to which the Director shall give full effect to such directions.
  3. Authorised officer
    1. Any officer of the National Migration Agency serving shall qualify as an authorised officer, and shall cease to be an authorised officer upon the termination of service with the National Migration Agency, or where a conflict of interest may arise owing to external relationships.
  4. Visa approval
    1. All visa applications seconded by the representative consular officer of the diplomatic mission in which the visa application was made shall be referred to an authorised officer for vetting.
    2. In the case of a renewal of an expired or expiring visa, the application shall be made within Imperial Royal Union to another authorised officer who shall act in the capacity of the representative consular officer.
    3. The representative consular officer shall ensure that each visa application contains:
      1. the completed visa application form in which the composition and contents of such form is determined by the Director, signed and endorsed by the applicant and the representative consular officer;
      2. a copy of a locally issued identity document that is not a passport, clearly stating the full name and address of the applicant inclusive of other personal particulars;
      3. a copy of a valid passport in which the applicant will use to gain entry into Imperial Royal Union upon the grant of visa;
      4. the relevant supporting documents required for the visa application as determined by the Director; and
      5. other supplementary documents required or advised by the representative consular officer to be furnished to provide for an efficient vetting process.
    4. The authorised officer shall use as many tools and take as much time as is reasonable at his disposal to ascertain that the information provided in a visa application is factually accurate, and should he discover that the information furnished is inaccurate, and if the authorised officer:
      1. believes that the cause of an inaccuracy is due to an honest mistake on the part of the applicant or the mistake or negligence of the representative consular officer, he shall allow the applicant to resubmit a corrected application within 10 working days and refer the matter in writing to his immediate superior for further action; or
      2. discovers that the inaccuracy was fraudulent in nature or an intentional act on the part of the applicant, he shall reject the application and refer the matter in writing to his immediate superior for further action.
    5. The authorised officer shall provide notice on the outcome of the visa application by delivering the notice to the respective diplomatic mission within 10 working days which shall then deliver to or have the applicant collect the notice as soon as is possible, and in the case of a visa renewal, by delivering the notice to the applicant within 5 working days.
    6. The delivery of notices may be made to the applicant in person, the applicant’s mailbox, or to an approved proxy of the applicant. Electronic delivery of notices shall only be supplementary and for the convenience of the applicant, and at the discretion of the authorised officer.
    7. Where the application has been rejected, the applicant may appeal the outcome within 30 days of the receipt of the notice, in which the immediate superior of the authorised officer responsible for the initial application shall review the grounds for review within 10 working days of receipt of the appeal and deliver a notice on the outcome of the review in accordance with paragraphs 5 and 6 of this section.
    8. The outcome of the review on each appeal is final and the Director may impose an administrative fee to be borne by the applicant on such reviews.
    9. An applicant shall submit no more than one application per year, unless a signed written order from the Director is obtained authorising the applicant to make another application in that year.
  5. Periodic review of approved visas
    1. The Director shall establish a separate office of authorised officers for the periodic review of all approved visas, to be conducted on an annual basis for all visas exceeding 18 months of stay in Imperial Royal Union.
    2. An authorised officer conducting a periodic review shall arrange with the visa holder to submit to a recorded interview with the authorised officer at the time and convenience of both the authorised officer and the visa holder, in which the visa holder shall be required to furnish all relevant information as may be requested by the authorised officer for such a review.
    3. Evidence obtained in the recorded interview may be admissible in any proceedings against the visa holder.
    4. The authorised officer shall complete the review within 10 working days of the recorded interview and submit his findings in a report to his immediate superior, and where there are sufficient grounds for the cancellation or revocation of the visa, he shall arrange for a meeting with the visa holder at any public security station as expedient as is possible to proceed with the cancellation or revocation of the visa in the presence of another public security officer as a witness.
  6. Maintenance requirement
    1. In the course of vetting of a visa application, the authorised officer shall ensure that the following conditions stipulated under this section (“the maintenance requirement”) have been adhered to and that the applicant is likely to be in compliance of such conditions upon the grant of visa.
    2. Any visa holder, at the time of a periodic review, who fails to meet the maintenance requirement, shall have his visa cancelled or revoked.
    3. The burden of proof to meet the maintenance requirement lies with the applicant or visa holder, where any representation made as proof of ability to meet the maintenance requirement may be admitted as evidence in any proceedings against the applicant or visa holder.
    4. The conditions of the maintenance requirements require that the applicant or visa holder:
      1. has in his bank account sufficient liquid savings to cover for 6 months-worth of personal expenses, assessed based on the median personal expense within Imperial Royal Union;
      2. has secured housing or has no difficulty in meeting housing rental or mortgage payment for the next 6 months after taking into the personal expenses;
      3. has in his bank account or that of his dependents sufficient liquid savings to cover for 3 months-worth of personal expenses of each dependent in his care, assessed based on the median personal expense of dependents within Imperial Royal Union;
      4. has not incurred debt amounting to more than 20% of liquid savings in his and his dependent's bank accounts combined; and
      5. is not a litigant in any active civil proceedings against him;
      6. has not been convicted of any criminal charges and is not an accused in any criminal proceedings against him with a combined sentence amounting to more than 30 days imprisonment or more than IRE 5,000 in fines; and
      7. is able to furnish to an authorised officer factual information suggesting that the original grounds for the grant of visa have not significantly changed or that the activities in which he is a participant of is not radically different from the activities allowed under the visa.
  7. Work visa
    1. A work visa may be granted for the purpose of allowing a foreign national the right to work within Imperial Royal Union.
    2. A work visa shall be granted for no longer than 3 years or for the duration of the employment contract in which the visa application was made with, whichever is shorter.
    3. A work visa automatically expires upon the termination of the employment contract in which the visa application was made with.
    4. The Director shall determine the reporting procedure for employers with employees under work visas and may determine if the employers are responsible for the visa status of their employees.
    5. A work visa holder shall not be barred from seeking and applying for job opportunities and new work visas outside of their working hours while on their current work visa.
    6. Prospective employers of persons requiring work visas shall provide a confirmed job offer prior to the application of the work visa.
  8. Education visa
    1. An education visa may be granted for the purpose of allowing a foreign national access to education within Imperial Royal Union, from primary education up to post-graduate education.
    2. Prospective education visa applicants shall submit a copy of their letter of admission into a local education institution, in which the institution shall then submit their own internal reference of admission of the visa applicant based on the procedure and timeline specified by the Director.
    3. An education visa holder may be allowed the right to work a limited number of hours per week as may be determined by the Director in conjunction with the relevant authorities on employment along with the maximum rate of remuneration for such work, and with the written permission of the education institution.
    4. An education visa automatically expires upon the completion of studies or if the visa holder has been expelled from the education institution.
  9. Medical visa
    1. A medical visa may be granted for the purpose of allowing a foreign national the right to extended stay within Imperial Royal Union for medical treatment.
    2. A medical visa application shall be supported by a referral letter from the local public hospital of the visa applicant and a letter of acceptance from a public hospital within Imperial Royal Union, in which the head of the department responsible for providing the specialised medical care to the visa applicant in both public hospitals shall endorse both letters.
    3. A medical visa applicant may seek medical care outside of a public hospital in Imperial Royal Union with a letter of referral endorsed by the Director of Medical Services and head of the department responsible for providing the specialised medical care within the public hospital in Imperial Royal Union for treatment at a private hospital in Imperial Royal Union.
    4. A medical visa may be granted to both the visa applicant and the accompanying caretaker.
    5. A medical visa may be indefinite in duration but shall automatically expire upon death of the visa holder or sufficient recovery of the visa holder, as determined by the attending physician of the visa holder in accordance with the reporting procedure and timeline stipulated by the Director.
    6. The Director may seek the second opinion of a qualified physician from another public hospital in Imperial Royal Union to verify representations made in the referral process or in periodic reviews.
  10. Dependents of visa holders
    1. A visa holder may bring with him into Imperial Royal Union his dependents, who shall be the current, lawful spouse and children aged under 18 under his legal care, for work visas exceeding 2 years of stay.
    2. The spouse of a visa holder may apply for a work visa while in Imperial Royal Union, where the outcome of the work visa application of the spouse is independent of the spouse's dependent status.
    3. The spouse and children of a visa holder may apply for an education visa while in Imperial Royal Union where the outcome of the education visa application of the spouse or children is independent of their dependent status.
    4. The Director may impose reporting procedures on dependents of visa holders during their first year of stay within Imperial Royal Union as dependents and direct authorised officers to conduct checks on the activities of dependents in the first year of stay. Dependents may be subjected to the same provisions as visa holders during the periodic review, in which they may also be interviews.
  11. Permanent residency
    1. Any person who has ordinarily resided in Imperial Royal Union as a dependent or a visa holder in the previous 10 consecutive years and with an age of at least 18 years old may apply to be a permanent resident.
    2. Any person who is a permanent resident shall be guaranteed the right of abode, work, education, and healthcare within Imperial Royal Union inclusive of any subsidies provided with regards to such rights, and any other rights that may be conferred upon permanent residents by any other written law in force, in which permanent residents shall also be subject to written law in force pertaining to their rights and obligations on tax and social insurance.
    3. Dependents of permanent residents remain as dependents, unless they were born after the person has been a permanent resident for at least 5 years, in which they shall inherit the status of permanent resident automatically, whereby the parents of these permanent residents shall make the necessary arrangements with their home state of the citizenship status of their child.
    4. Any person who is a permanent resident may surrender their rights as a permanent resident for returnable social insurance monies made under their previous obligations as a permanent resident, following which they cease to be a permanent resident.
  12. Application for permanent residency
    1. The Director shall establish a separate office of authorised officers for processing of applications for permanent residency.
    2. An authorised officer processing an application for permanent residency shall not be the same authorised officer in the processing of an application for a visa or for the periodic review of such visa for the same applicant.
    3. An application for permanent residency, upon fulfilment of the basic requirement under section 11 of this Act and the maintenance requirement, shall be supported with character reference by a citizen of Imperial Royal Union, and the endorsement of the cantonal migration and residency authorities of at least 3 Cantons in which the applicant foresees himself residing in.
    4. Cantonal migration and residency authorities may prescribe their own procedures for applications and impose the necessary administrative fees at their own discretion.
    5. The Director may impose additional procedures for applications and shall impose an administrative fee to cover the administrative costs of the application.
    6. An applicant shall be required to demonstrate his ability to communicate verbally and in writing without assistance from any other individual in at least 2 of the 3 official languages of Imperial Royal Union.
    7. An authorised officer processing such an application shall arrange with the applicant to submit to a recorded interview with the authorised officer at the time and convenience of both the authorised officer and the applicant, in which the applicant shall be required to furnish all relevant information as may be requested by the authorised officer for his vetting.
    8. Evidence obtained in the recorded interview may be admissible in any proceedings against the applicant.
    9. Upon the completion of vetting by the authorised officer, the authorised officer shall notify the applicant within 10 working days by written notice of the completion of vetting if the authorised officer has rejected the application, and where the authorised officer has approved the application, a second opinion shall be sought from another authorised officer. Where both authorised officers have approved the application, the applicant shall be notified within 10 working days by written notice of the completion of vetting the outcome of his application.
    10. Where permanent residency has been granted, the applicant's status as a permanent resident shall come into effect on the first day of the sixth month after the approval of permanent residency, and during the transition period as a visa holder or dependent of a visa holder, shall have their visa or dependent status extended automatically for that duration.
    11. An application for permanent residency may be made only thrice in a person's lifetime, with at least 5 years between each application for permanent residency.
    12. The Director may revoke the permanent resident status of any permanent resident on the same grounds in which a visa holder may have his visa cancelled or revoked, subject to further investigation by an authorised officer on behalf of the Director.
  13. Former citizens and permanent residents
    1. A former citizen or permanent resident of Imperial Royal Union may apply for permanent residency upon renunciation of citizenship or annulment of permanent residency.
    2. A former citizen or permanent resident of Imperial Royal Union shall be exempted from the visa requirements prior to the application of permanent residency, but shall be required to surrender half of all social insurance monies withdrawn at the point of renunciation or annulment, inclusive of the interest accumulated on such monies, in addition to other requirements and procedures stipulated by the Director.
    3. A former citizen of Imperial Royal Union who holds dual citizenship in any two nations shall not be eligible for permanent residency.
    4. A former citizen or permanent resident of Imperial Royal Union who did not, prior to renunciation or annulment, fulfil or complete in full their civic obligations as may have been required at the time they were a citizen or permanent resident shall not be eligible for permanent residency, unless otherwise assessed by the Director for special considerations and at the discretion and procedures stipulated by the Director.
