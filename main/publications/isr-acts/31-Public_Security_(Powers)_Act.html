ISR Cap. 31
In Force

Public Security (Powers) Act

  1. Short title and commencement
    1. This Act may be cited as the "Public Security (Powers) Act".
    2. The following provisions of this Act are effective as of 17 April 2021.
  2. Powers subject to review by appropriate authority
    1. In the exercise of the powers granted to a public security officer, the appropriate authority shall be required to approve in written order or verbal acknowledgement the exercise of such powers, unless otherwise temporarily empowered by a direct written order of the appropriate authority or any other higher authority on the exercise of such powers.
    2. The review of powers may be retroactive, depending on the exigency of circumstances prompting the exercise of such powers.
    3. Where the appropriate authority retroactively deems the exercise of such powers as unjustified within the context of the circumstances prompting the exercise of such powers, or where a public security officer has exercised such powers outside of his jurisdictional limits without prior authorisation, the appropriate authority shall refer the public security officer and his findings for further review by an internal committee and an independent authority within the Directorate-General.
  3. Use of force
    1. A public security officer may use force to restrain, prevent or stop an offence from being committed, with or without the use of tools of non-lethal means at his disposal.
    2. The use of force shall be reasonable and proportional to the offence being committed.
    3. The use of force shall be applied only after a verbal warning issued by the public security officer and the display, in part or in full, the warrant of authority.
    4. The use of force may be used by an officer to effect an arrest.
  4. Use of lethal force
    1. As a preliminary to the use of lethal force, the conditions required in the use of force must first be present.
    2. The use of lethal force shall only be justified in instances of self-defence, and to prevent severe harm or further loss of life.
    3. Where lethal force is used, the appropriate authority shall refer the public security officer for further review by an internal committee and an independent authority within the Directorate-General, during which the public security officer shall be suspended from further duties until otherwise noticed by the internal committee through the appropriate authority.
    4. Where a review finds the use of lethal force as unjustified by both the internal committee and independent authority, and upon the acceptance of the findings of such review by the Deputy Director-General for Administration, the public security officer shall bear no immunity and civil liability claims and criminal prosecution.
  5. Questioning of persons
    1. Every person, when questioned by a public security officer, shall be required to furnish the details and information required by the officer as accurately as possible, without reservation or concealment.
    2. Any person who knowingly furnishes to an officer inaccurate information for the purposes of concealment or with intent to mislead shall be guilty of an offence relating to the obstruction of justice.
    3. An officer shall always make known to the person being questioned of their identity and clearly display their warrant of authority for the duration of the questioning, and upon request of the person being questioned, provide information pertaining to the rank, name, and unit of the questioning officer.
  6. Arrest
    1. A public security officer may effect an arrest on a person if:
      1. the officer is of the belief that an offence may be committed by the person;
      2. the officer has witnessed an offence being committed by the person;
      3. the person, when approached, questioned, or ordered by the officer, is unable to provide a satisfactory response to the officer or makes an attempt to evade or flee from the officer;
      4. the person, in the course of interaction with the officer, knowingly or unknowingly furnishes to the officer an admission of guilt or knowledge of an offence being committed;
      5. the person knowingly resists or ignores the orders of the officer; or
      6. the person is presented with a signed warrant of arrest by an investigative authority.
    2. When an arrest is to be effected by the public security officer, the officer shall inform the person under arrest the rank, name and unit which will identify the officer, alongside an indication to the warrant of authority, and the justification for arrest based on the aforementioned reasons.
    3. Where an arrest has grounds in an offence being committed and in the absence of a warrant of arrest, the arresting officer shall clearly state the offence in which the person is suspected to have committed.
    4. The arresting officer shall inform the person under arrest of his right to legal representation and right against self-incrimination, in accordance with written law in force at the point of arrest and any other rights and provisions that may be specified by such written law.
    5. Throughout the process of arrest, the arresting officer shall have full discretion regarding the choice of restraint or concealment, if any, provided the arrest is on lawful grounds and in accordance with the aforementioned provisions of this Section.
  7. Entry into property
    1. A public security officer may enter any private property if the officer:
      1. has been invited into the property;
      2. is responding to a probable emergency within the property;
      3. bears witness to an offence being committed in the property; or
      4. has a valid warrant which may only be served by entry into the property;
    2. Where an officer is of the belief that an offence is being committed in the property but is not a witness to such an offence or is unable to secure in his possession any evidence to suggest such an offence, the officer shall seek the approval of the appropriate authority prior to entry into the property.
    3. Where approval is sought from the appropriate authority, the appropriate authority is equally liable as the officer who enters the property should the entry be deemed as unjustified upon the inquiry of the internal committee and the independent authority.
  8. Searching of property
    1. Where an offence has been committed and a search of the relevant premises and properties of the offender is required in order to secure evidence for the prosecution of such an offender, the public security officer shall be required to furnish a search warrant to the offender signed by the appropriate authority for the search of such premises and properties.
    2. Where the search is to be carried out on a public property or State land, no warrant shall be required.
    3. Where a search of a property is to be carried out on a private property, the offender and the owner of such private property shall be present to witness such a search.
    4. Where the offender or the owner of such property is unable to be present during the search of the property, the search of the property shall be accompanied by a representative of an independent body and fully documented by videography for the duration of the search.
    5. An officer with justifiable cause for entry into a property shall not conduct any search of the property without a search warrant unless it is conducted for the expeditious securement or preservation of evidence and in the presence of another officer with acknowledgement by the appropriate authority.
  9. Body search
    1. Where an officer is of the belief or is made to belief that a person may have in his possession any item which may be deemed as evidence or unlawful, regardless of the factualness of the circumstances, the officer may proceed to conduct a body search on such person.
    2. For the purposes of this section, possession may be defined as the carry of an item within the clothing of a person, within the accessories that may be worn and removed from a person, within any cavity, natural or unnatural, of the body of a person, and any other possible circumstance in which an item may be physically found in or on a person.
    3. A body search shall be carried out by an officer of the same sex or gender of the person being searched, unless otherwise consented by the person being searched.
    4. A body search shall be carried out only in the presence of at least one other officer of the same sex or gender of the person being searched, unless otherwise consented by the person being searched.
    5. A body search shall be carried out when fully documented by videography, unless otherwise consented by the person being searched.
    6. Upon effecting at arrest, an officer shall subject the arrested person to a body search for the purposes of securing evidence or for the safety of the officer, which shall be conducted at the point of arrest or upon delivering the arrested person to a holding facility, at the discretion of the arresting officer, in accordance with the aforementioned provisions of this Section.
  10. Request for identification
    1. Any person approached by a public security officer, after having been identified as a public security officer, with a request for identification shall fully comply and produce their government-issued identity document.
    2. An officer may effect an arrest on such a person who refuses to comply in the display of any government-issued identity document in their possession.
    3. All government-issued identity documents shall be considered as valid and compliant, unless otherwise specified by the officer to be of certain format or medium.
  11. Exemption traffic offences whilst on duty
    1. All public security officers whilst on duty and having identified themselves as public security officers shall bear immunity against prosecution for traffic offences.
    2. The provisions of paragraph (1) of this section shall not apply should the appropriate authority deem the traffic offence caused whilst on public duty to be unjustified or unnecessary.
