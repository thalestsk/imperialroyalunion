ISR Cap. 2
In Force

Estates-General Act

  1. Short title and commencement
    1. This Act may be cited as the "Estates-General Act".
    2. The following provisions of this Act are effective as of 10 April 2021.
  2. Powers of the Estates-General
    1. The Estates-General shall exercise legislative authority as may be deferred to it by the Emperor.
  3. Composition of the Estates-General
    1. The Estates-General shall comprise of the Senate as the Upper House and the Chamber of Deputies (“Chamber”) as the Lower House. Each house shall have the following composition:
      1. Members of the Senate ("Senators") shall be appointed by the Emperor. The Emperor may appoint any number of Senators as deemed necessary.
      2. Members of the Chamber ("Deputies") shall be appointed by the Cantonal Governments. Each Canton shall be represented by 11 Deputies.
      3. The Emperor shall appoint a non-Deputy to the Chamber as the Chief Presiding Officer.
  4. Appointment of Deputies
    1. Deputies may be appointed by the Cantonal Governments or the Governor-Generals, or by election by the Cantonal legislatures or the people of the Cantons, or a hybrid appointment system consisting of the above methods.
    2. Deputies shall be residents of the Canton in which they are representative of.
    3. Deputies who are elected shall not have resigned or been removed from elected public office before the end of their tenure in elected public office.
    4. Deputies shall not hold any other office or undertake any employment during their tenure as Deputy, unless otherwise prescribed by the Cantonal Governments, Governor-Generals or Cantonal legislatures.
  5. Powers of the Senate
    1. Whereby deferred to it by the Emperor, the Senate shall have the authority to:
      1. originate and pass Acts of Estates;
      2. originate and pass Revenue Orders and Supply Orders;
      3. remove Lord Administrators and Governor-Generals from office at the recommendation of the Chamber; and
      4. nominate candidates to the Emperor for appointment to the Senate.
  6. Conduct of business in the Senate
    1. The Senate shall be organised by the Emperor, and Senators shall be accorded privileges as it may deem to be necessary.
    2. The Senate shall operate in private or public sittings as it may deem to be necessary.
    3. All decisions undertaken in the Senate shall be supported by a two-thirds majority.
  7. Powers of the Chamber
    1. The Chamber shall be accorded limited legislative authority and serve a consultative role. It shall have the authority to:
      1. discuss petitions and draft responses to petitions;
      2. originate, discuss and review Acts of Estates;
      3. discuss and review Revenue Orders and Supply Orders;
      4. arrange and conduct Sessions of Inquiry into issues of national public concern as may be ordered by the Emperor;
      5. suspend the powers of any Cantonal Government and the Cantonal legislatures at the advice of the Governor-General and Lord Administrator;
      6. review and revoke laws passed by the Cantonal Governments and the Cantonal legislatures; and
      7. interpret, review, pass and revoke amendments to constitutional laws of the Overseas Territories.
    2. Where the power has been conferred upon the Chamber to discuss or review but not pass law, the Senate shall consider the recommendations and findings of the Chamber in its deliberative process.
  8. Conduct of business in the Chamber
    1. The Chamber shall be presided over by the Chief Presiding Officer.
    2. The privileges that may be accorded to a Deputy shall be subject to the approval of the Senate, of which the Chamber may make such recommendations to the Senate.
    3. All decisions undertaken in the Chamber shall be supported by a simple majority comprising of more than half the Deputies. Deputies shall not abstain from voting.
    4. Where the power has been conferred upon the Chamber to pass or revoke law, the Chamber shall organize a committee to discuss the exercise of such power before a plenary session may be called.
    5. The quorum for the Chamber may be achieved by any of the following, whereby:
      1. more than four-sevenths of the total number of Deputies are present;
      2. at least 6 Deputies from all Cantons are present; or
      3. all Deputies from any 26 Cantons are present;
  9. The Chief Presiding Officer
    1. The Chief Presiding Officer ranks behind all Senators and ahead of all Deputies.
    2. The Chief Presiding Officer shall have the authority to:
      1. draft and amend the Chamber Standing Orders;
      2. enforce and ensure compliance to the Chamber Standing Orders;
      3. arrange the order of business in the Chamber;
      4. invite non-Deputies to speak or provide second opinions in the Chamber at the recommendation of more than half the Deputies of the Chamber;
      5. appoint Deputy Presiding Officers from amongst Deputies nominated by the Chamber to carry out the duties of the Chief Presiding Officer in his absence and where delegated;
      6. appoint and dismiss the administrative and support staff of the Chamber; and
      7. form and dissolve committees, where the Chamber shall nominate and elect Deputies to sit on the committee, with the Chief Presiding Officer appointing the committee’s chairman from amongst the Deputies elected to sit on the committee.
  10. Legislative precedence
    1. The order of legislative precedence is prescribed in the First Schedule of this Act.
