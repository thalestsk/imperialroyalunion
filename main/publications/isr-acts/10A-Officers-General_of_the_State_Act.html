ISR Cap. 10A
In Force

Officers-General of the State Act

  1. Short title and commencement
    1. This Act may be cited as the "Officers-General of the State Act".
    2. The following provisions of this Act are effective as of 11 October 2022.
  2. Officers-General
    1. The provisions of this Act apply solely to the appointments and the corresponding titles listed within this Act.
    2. Such appointments and titles shall be collectively referred to as "Officers-General".
    3. The Officers-General shall be higher on the order of precedence over other Public Service Officers of the same grade.
    4. The right to appointment of Officers-General lie solely with the Emperor.
    5. The rights and powers of the Officers-General are conferred by virtue of their appointment as an Officer-General, and do not rest with the body from which they discharge their functions.
    6. The right to appointment and the powers of all subordinate officers of the Officers-General lie solely with the respective Officers-General.
    7. The powers of the Officers-General shall be enjoined by this Act, and any other written law in force conferring further powers and obligations to an Officer-General.
  3. Registrar-General
    1. The powers conferred to the Registrar-General shall be to:
      1. maintain an official record of instruments of law of HIM Government;
      2. maintain an official record of registries required for government and business efficacy; and
      3. verify and clarify doubts on the authenticity and efficacy of such records.
  4. Attorney-General
    1. The powers conferred to the Attorney-General shall be to:
      1. represent HIM Government before the judiciary;
      2. advise HIM Government on legal matters;
      3. prosecute offenders as per statutory law;
      4. assist in the drafting of instruments of law; and
      5. creating a conducive environment for the development of law and the legal business.
  5. Heralder-General
    1. The powers conferred to the Heralder-General shall be to:
      1. control and oversee the use of styles, honours, and titles within Imperial Royal Union;
      2. advise the Emperor on the issuance and revocation of such styles, honours, and titles; and
      3. evaluate and review the privileges and rights of title holders.
  6. Procurer-General
    1. The powers conferred to the Procurer-General shall be to:
      1. form business relationships with providers of regular government supplies;
      2. negotiate and review contracts for the pruchase of such supplies; and
      3. oversee and report on irregular or imprudent purchases of such supplies by HIM Government.
  7. Accountant-General
    1. The powers conferred to the Accountant-General shall be to:
      1. maintain an official record of the financial statement of HIM Government;
      2. analyse and optimise treasury asset holdings and movements; and
      3. determine regulatory standards for financial accounting.
  8. Trustee-General
    1. The powers conferred to the Trustee-General shall be to:
      1. administer the estates of a deceased person;
      2. regulate and assess compensations for torts; and
      3. manage monies under public trusteeship.
  9. Comptroller-General
    1. The powers conferred to the Comptroller-General shall be to:
      1. implement tax policy and collect revenue monies;
      2. enforce against errant parties relating to revenue matters; and
      3. consolidate and prepare all revenue monies for accounting and disbursement.
  10. Hydrographer-General
    1. The powers conferred to the Hydrographer-General shall be to:
      1. survey, map, and model the marine geology and environment;
      2. identify, determine, and maintain the maritime routes of navigation; and
      3. manage built-environment assets within, under, and above the nation's water bodies.
  11. Surveyor-General
    1. The powers conferred to the Surveyor-General shall be to:
      1. survey, map, and model the terranean geology and environment;
      2. provide and maintain cartographical and topological frameworks; and
      3. authorise and oversee exploratory and research missions of the land.
  12. Rector-General
    1. The powers conferred to the Rector-General shall be to:
      1. determine the means of and implement standardised testing;
      2. issue and award the recognition of qualifications; and
      3. develop the education curriculum framework.
  13. Physician-General
    1. The powers conferred to the Physician-General shall be to:
      1. determine and develop the training and formation programme of medical practitioners;
      2. licence, regulate, and oversee the medical practice and allied health professions;
      3. formulate and implement policy for the holistic development of the medical community;
      4. protect and uphold the reputation and standards of the medical profession.
