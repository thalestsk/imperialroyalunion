ISR Cap. 1
In Force

Instrument of Government 2020

  1. Short title and commencement
    1. This Act may be cited as the "Instrument of Government 2020".
    2. The following provisions of this Instrument are effective as of 7 November 2020.
  2. The Emperor
    1. His Imperial Majesty the Emperor of Imperial Royal Union ("the Emperor") is the sovereign authority and is the supreme executive, legislative and judicial authority in Imperial Royal Union.
  3. Primacy of Instrument of Government
    1. Where any law, be it written, oral, customary or by judicial precedent, may be in contrary to the provisions of this Instrument in intention, operation or function, this Instrument shall take precedence.
    2. This Instrument shall only be amended or repealed at the discretion of the Emperor.
  4. His Imperial Majesty's Government
    1. The Emperor may appoint and dismiss officers to exercise his authority on his behalf. The body of officers that form up the executive branch of the central government, inclusive of the Emperor, shall be collectively referred to as “HIM Government”.
    2. All local authorities, regardless of the level of the administrative division, shall be subordinate to HIM Government.
    3. All powers not delegated to local authorities by written order are retained by HIM Government.
    4. All powers delegated to local authorities may be subject to revision in scope, authority and jurisdiction by HIM Government.
  5. The Estates-General
    1. The collective body of legislators appointed by the Emperor for the exercise of legislative authority shall be the Estates-General.
  6. The Supreme Court of Judicature
    1. The collective body of justices appointed by the Emperor for the exercise of judicial authority shall be the Supreme Court of Judicature.
  7. Administrative divisions

      8. Imperial Royal Union shall be administratively sub-divided into Territories of the following order of precedence:

      1. Port Imperial Capital Territory,
      2. Territory of Islandus,
      3. Territory of Imperialia,
      4. Eastern Territory,
      5. Southern Isles Territory,
      6. the Overseas Territories in order of accession to Imperial Royal Union.
    1. Territories shall be further sub-divided into Cantons with the exception of Port Imperial Capital Territory and the Overseas Territories.
    2. Cantons shall be further sub-divided into Metropolitans Areas and Prefectures. Each Canton shall only comprise of one Metropolitan Area and any number of Prefectures as may be required for governance or planning purposes.
    3. Metropolitan Areas and Prefectures may be further sub-divided by local authorities.
  8. Territorial Administrative Authorities
    1. Each Territory, with the exception of Port Imperial Capital Territory and the Overseas Territories, shall be collectively governed by a Territorial Administrative Authority.
    2. The Territorial Administrative Authorities shall have the power to:
      1. define, delimit and modify the borders and boundaries of the Territory or its subordinated Cantons in concurrence with HIM Government and the adjacent Territories;
      2. draft, amend and repeal electoral laws pertaining to the election of persons to public office of the Cantons within the Territory;
      3. manage and maintain land owned by the State within the Territory with the permission of HIM Government;
      4. maintain a contingent force for the provision, supplementation, augmentation and coordination of emergency services;
      5. organise, incorporate and dissolve bodies corporate for the promotion of economic activity and provision of supplementary public services within the Territory;
      6. build, demolish, maintain and plan transport, telecommunications, utilities and housing infrastructure within the Territory with permission from the Cantons,
      7. implement other laws and exercise other powers as may be devolved or deferred to the Territorial Administrative Authority by HIM Government or the Cantons.
  9. Lord Administrators
    1. Each Territorial Administrative Authority shall be managed by a Lord Administrator. The Lord Administrator shall be an elected officer who shall exercise the powers of the Territorial Administrative Authority. The election of the Lord Administrator shall fulfil the following requirements:
      1. The Lord Administrator shall be a resident of the Territory where he is serving as Lord Administrator;
      2. The candidate for Lord Administrator shall be subject to the assent of the Emperor;
      3. The Lord Administrator shall be elected by electoral college, with the college consisting of electors from the Cantons within the Territory, and with HIM Government determining the number of electors in each Canton. The result of the popular vote within each Canton shall determine the candidate in which the electors of each Canton shall vote for.
      4. Elections for the Lord Administrator shall be called every five years, and shall take place on the eleventh day of November.
    2. The Lord Administrator shall have the power to appoint and dismiss officers to exercise the functions of the Territorial Administrative Authority.
    3. In the event of the resignation, death or incapacitation of a Lord Administrator resulting in the vacancy of the position or the incumbent Lord Administrator being unable to discharge his duties, the Emperor may appoint a Lord Administrator in an acting capacity for the discharge of such duties in the interim until to the next election.
  10. Cantonal Governments
    1. Each Canton shall form its own Cantonal Government, comprising of the local executive authority and legislative authority. The composition of the local executive authority shall be determined by the local laws of the Canton as decided by the local legislative authority. Where officers of the Cantonal Government are elected, the electoral process shall be determined by the respective Territorial Administrative Authority.
  11. Governor-Generals
    1. The head of each Canton shall be the Governor-General and shall serve as the resident representative of the Emperor in the Canton. The Governor-General may be granted more powers and duties as may be required by the Cantonal Government and where the Cantonal Government requires the Governor-General to be an elected officer, the electoral process shall be determined by the respective Territorial Administrative Authority.
