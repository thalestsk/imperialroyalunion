ISR Cap. 30
In Force

Public Security (Organisation) Act

  1. Short title and commencement
    1. This Act may be cited as the "Public Security (Organisation) Act".
    2. The following provisions of this Act are effective as of 14 May 2021.
  2. Directorate-General of Public Security
    1. The Directorate-General of Public Security (“Directorate-General”) shall be the overarching umbrella organisation to which all public security departments are subordinate to.
  3. Public security
    1. A public security department shall be defined as a public entity established by either HIM Government, the Territorial Administrative Authorities, or the Cantonal Governments for the purposes of law enforcement and for the provision of public security and order.
    2. A public security officer shall be defined as a full-time employee of a public security department, having been appropriately trained and empowered by a public security department to carry out law enforcement responsibilities as ordered, directed, and required by the Directorate-General or their respective public security department.
  4. Appropriate authority
    1. Each and every public security officer is accountable to an appropriate authority, which may be defined as the persons or officers responsible for their appointment or confirmation, or as their immediate superior or supervisor, or any officer of at least 1 rank superior designated as the appropriate authority within the Directorate-General and its subordinate public security departments.
  5. Director-General of Public Security
    1. The Director-General of Public Security, hereafter “Director-General”, shall be the head of the Directorate-General of Public Security and by extension the provisional commander-in-chief of the public security force.
    2. The Director-General shall be appointed by the Emperor and shall be a public security officer in active service at the time of appointment.
    3. The Director-General shall be accorded the powers accorded to his immediate deputies and any other powers as may be determined by the Emperor.
  6. Deputy Directors-General
    1. The 3 immediate deputies to the Director-General shall receive the appointment of Deputy Director-Generals, suffixed with the relevant scope of their responsibilities to which they have been accorded the powers to fulfil such responsibilities.
    2. The Director-General shall nominate public security officers in active service at the time of nomination, subject to the confirmation of the Emperor, for the 3 Deputy Directors-General.
    3. The 3 broad scopes of responsibilities to which a Deputy Director-General may be responsible for shall be as follows:
      1. Administration – in which the Deputy Director-General appointed shall take charge of matters of policy, resource management, future development, organisational planning, state and public accountability, and the professional services as may be required by the Directorate-General of Public Security;
      2. Frontline Police – in which the Deputy Director-General appointed shall coordinate the national policing effort through the provision of guidance to the leadership of all public security departments performing frontline policing and crime investigation duties, and in due course assume command when appropriate; and
      3. Homeland Security – in which the Deputy Director-General appointed shall take charge of the management of the specialist public security departments empowered for national investigations and crime prevention, including the departments responsible for the enforcement of immigration policy, customs policy, and rehabilitative policy.
    4. The Deputy Directors-General may appoint their immediate deputies and the heads of the public security departments under their purview when a vacancy arises from the creation of a new office or through resignation of such deputy or head or by revocation of such appointment.
    5. The Deputy Directors-General shall not directly appoint other subordinate officers to their immediate deputies and the heads of the public security departments under their purview but may subject such appointments to further confirmation at their own discretion, unless otherwise prescribed by any other written law in force at the time of appointment or confirmation.
    6. The Deputy Directors-General shall not exercise their power to assume command of frontline operations without the explicit consent of the Director-General, seconded by their immediate deputy and at least one other Deputy Director-General, and to similar effect, the Director-General shall not exercise any power to assume command of frontline operations should no Deputy Director-General exercise such power, and when such power is exercised, that all 3 Deputy Directors-General are in accord with the Director-General, unless otherwise ordered by the Emperor.
  7. Warrant of authority
    1. No public security officer shall discharge their duties without bearing on themselves a warrant of authority indicative of their empowerment to exercise such authority.
    2. The warrant of authority shall comprise of an identification document issued by the Directorate-General and a badge issued by their respective public security department.
    3. The warrant of authority may be revoked by the appropriate authority.
    4. The warrant of authority shall be subject to the jurisdictional limits of the issuing public security department, unless otherwise authorised by the Director-General or a Deputy Director-General.
  8. Jurisdictional limits
    1. Public security officers directly subordinate to the Directorate-General, unless otherwise authorised by the by the Director-General or a Deputy Director-General, shall not exercise any powers of law enforcement without such explicit authorization within the written law of the Canton in which they may exercise such powers.
    2. Public security officers subordinate to national police units, which shall be public security departments under the Deputy Director-General responsible for Homeland Security, shall have their jurisdiction limited to the scope of responsibilities held by their respective public security department but not limited to geopolitical boundaries.
    3. Public security officers subordinate to territorial police units, which shall be public security departments under the Deputy Director-General responsible for Frontline Police, shall have their jurisdiction limited to the respective Territory to which they are deployed to.
    4. Public security officers subordinate to local police units, which shall be public security departments under the Deputy Director-General responsible for Frontline Police, shall have their jurisdiction limited to the respective Canton to which they are deployed to.
    5. Heads of public security departments shall justify in writing to their respective Deputy Director-General, their reasons for temporarily lifting jurisdictional limits in allowing public security officers not subordinate to their department to exercise jurisdiction normally exercised by their department and be responsible for the issuance of warrants of authority in such instances.
  9. State and public accountability
    1. All public security departments are accountable to the Directorate-General and shall report on their work accordingly as such through their respective Deputy Director-General.
    2. Public security departments which may be defined as territorial police units shall be jointly accountable to their respective Territorial Administrative Authorities, from which they derive half their fiscal supply from and may be subject to further restrictions or authorisations on their jurisdiction and operational scope, with the exception of direct command authority, which shall be retained within the Directorate-General.
    3. Public security departments which may be defined as local police units shall be jointly accountable to their respective Cantonal Governments, from which they derive half their fiscal supply from and may be subject to further restrictions or authorisations on their jurisdiction and operational scope, with the exception of operational command authority, in which Cantonal Governments shall not assume or exercise such command without the recommendation of their respective Territorial Administrative Authority and at the confirmation of such recommendation by the Director-General.
  10. National police units
    1. The Deputy Director-General responsible for Homeland Security shall be required to maintain a public security department for each of the following basic national policing responsibilities, and may choose to maintain more units for other specialist national policing responsibilities fall outside of or within the scope of the following responsibilities:
      1. criminal investigations at the national level, such as crimes which may be committed internationally, or domestically beyond Territorial or Cantonal borders;
      2. enforcement of immigration, border, and checkpoint policy;
      3. implementation, operation and enforcement of rehabilitative policy; and
      4. enforcement of customs policy in accordance with import and export laws.
  11. Frontline police units
    1. The Deputy Director-General responsible for Frontline Police shall be required to maintain at least 1 public security department for each Territory and Canton, in which multiple Cantons may by assent of their respective Cantonal Legislatures aggregate the provision of law enforcement services into a single public security department.
  12. Ranks
