ISR Cap. 12
In Force

Supply and Appropriations Acts

  1. Short title and commencement
    1. This Act may be cited as the "Supply and Appropriations Acts".
    2. The following provisions of this Act are effective as of 25 April 2021.
  2. National reserves
    1. The monies resulting from a net surplus of each fiscal year shall be contributed to a consolidated pool of monies designated as the national Reserves.
    2. The Emperor may appoint one or several qualified professionals for the purposes of managing the investments made with the monies of the Reserves, in order to ensure a steady stream of revenue into the Reserves independent of government revenues.
  3. Framework for projected expediture
    1. HIM Government shall, under the advisement of the Public Revenue Administration, and in consultation with the Territorial Administrative Authorities, the Cantonal Governments, and all subordinate departments of HIM Government, devise a Projected Expenditure Framework to determine the total public expenditure required for a fiscal period extending to 5 years, in which the total sum of monies required for such a fiscal period shall be the 5-Year Projected Sum.
    2. The 5-Year Projected Sum shall be revised on annual basis, taking into account realized expenditure in the years elapsed prior to any revision of the 5-Year Projected Sum.
    3. Any revision on the 5-Year Projected Sum shall not exceed the annualized amount on the harmonic mean of the previous 3 revisions of the 5-Year Projected Sum by more than 20%.
  4. Reserve monies
    1. In the issuance of Appropriation Orders to account for the annual expenditure, HIM Government may withdraw up to 50% of the average net investment returns over a period of the preceding 5 years from the Reserves to supplement the collected revenue.
    2. Where the total annual revenue inclusive of investment returns exceeds the annualized amount on the harmonic mean of the previous 3 revisions of the 5-Year Projected Sum, HIM Government shall return 75% of all excess monies to the Reserves and shall either spend or return to the Cantonal Governments the sum of monies not returned to the Reserves.
    3. In the event of an economic crisis which may be precipitated by or lead to a currency shock, HIM Government may, for the purposes of avoiding, preventing, or mitigating such a shock for maintaining the state of global trade, withdraw additional monies from the Reserve for such measures.
    4. In the event of an economic crisis which may be precipitated by a global economic downturn, and where monies are to be allocated for fiscal stimulus packages not covered within the previous 3 revisions of the 5-Year Projected Sum, monies from the Reserves shall not be used for such stimuli.
  5. Public debt and borrowing
    1. HIM Government may borrow and incur debt by direct borrowing from financial institutions and the issuance of bonds to finance government expenditure.
    2. The outstanding total public debt incurred by HIM Government at any point in time shall not exceed 2% of the gross domestic product of Imperial Royal Union.
    3. The annual new public debt incurred by HIM Government in any fiscal year shall not exceed 0.25% of the gross domestic product of Imperial Royal Union nor exceed more than 50% of the combined annual new public debt from the last 3 years, unless the outstanding total public debt is less than 0.25% of the gross domestic product of Imperial Royal Union.
    4. HIM Government shall always borrow at least 50% of the projected costs on one-off infrastructure expenditure, such as infrastructure construction projects, but shall not borrow to finance recurring benefit transfer programmes, such as social security programmes.
    5. Where public debt has been incurred, HIM Government shall endeavour to fulfil such debt obligations by means of repayment within a period of 10 to 20 years.
  6. Principle of fiscal balance
    1. In the revision and creation of the 5-Year Projected Sum, HIM Government shall avoid running a surplus or deficit at the end of the fiscal period extending to 5 years to avoid inefficiencies with the prolonged servicing of debt or unspent surpluses at the expense of national welfare.
