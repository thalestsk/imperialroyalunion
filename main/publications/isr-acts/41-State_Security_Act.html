In Force

State Security Act

  1. Short title and commencement
    1. This Act may be cited as the "State Security Act".
    2. The following provisions of this Act are effective as of 3 December 2021.
  2. Directorate-General of State Security
    1. This Act establishes the Directorate-General of State Security ("Directorate-General") as the national organisation responsible for intelligence, espionage, surveillance and affiliated state security activities.
  3. State Security Organisations
    1. State Security Organisations shall be differentiated departments within the Directorate-General, each with its own specific function.
    2. A State Security Organisation shall be headed by a Director, assisted by a Deputy Director.
    3. The employees of a State Security Organisation, including the Director and Deputy Director, shall be deemed as employees of the Directorate-General.
  4. Director-General of State Security
    1. The Director-General of State Security ("Director-General") shall be appointed by the Emperor.
    2. The Director-General shall:
      1. appoint and dismiss officers to and from positions within the Directorate-General;
      2. develop and organise the Directorate-General in a manner which is expedient for the performance of its functions;
      3. plan, manage and secure the financial and operational requirements of the Directorate-General;
      4. coordinate between the State Security Organisations and maintain the efficiency of the Directorate-General;
      5. assess, evaluate, vet and nominate candidates for Directors and Deputy Directors of State Security Organisations for approval by the Emperor;
      6. update and report on the Directorate-General's activities and operations to the Emperor;
      7. supervise and oversee the operations of the State Security Organisations; and
      8. appoint Deputy Directors-General for the delegation of such responsibilities.
  5. Directors and Deputy Directors
    1. The scope and limitations to the powers of Directors and Deputy Directors, in the absence of provisions from any written law in force, shall be determined by the Director-General
    2. The authority of the Directors shall not exceed that of what is necessary for the performance of the functions of their respective State Security Organisations and shall be conducted in accordance with procedures provided within this Act and any other written law in force.
    3. Operations undertaken within the scope of functions of a State Security Organisation shall be undertaken with explicit written permission from either the Director or the Deputy Director, or any official who has been appointed by the Director to act on their behalf.
  6. Threats to national security
    1. The definition of a threat to national security of which the Directorate-General has every obligation to take action against shall encompass:
      1. espionage, sabotage, or activities in support of espionage and sabotage that is against or is detrimental to the interests of Imperial Royal Union;
      2. foreign influenced activities within or relating to Imperial Royal Union that are clandestine or deceptive or involve a threat to any citizen of Imperial Royal Union;
      3. activities within or relating to Imperial Royal Union directed toward or in support of the threat or use of acts of violence against persons or property for the purpose of achieving a political or ideological objective; and
      4. activities directed toward undermining by covert unlawful acts, or directed toward or intended ultimately to lead to the destruction of order within Imperial Royal Union.
  7. Department of Internal Security and Counterespionage
    1. The functions of the Department of Internal Security and Counterespionage ("DISC") shall be:
      1. to obtain intelligence about the capabilities, intentions and activities of persons or organisations within Imperial Royal Union from conventional and non-conventional methods;
      2. to prevent, disrupt and intervene in activities determined to constitute a threat to national security;
      3. to exercise the necessary special powers not available to public security officers as defined under the Public Order (Organisation) Act in order for the proper discharge of its functions under part 2 of this section; and
      4. to support and provide assistance to the Directorate-General of Public Security for the enforcement of law and the maintenance of public order.
  8. Department of Orbital and Terrestrial Analysis
    1. The functions of the Department of Orbital and Terrestrial Analysis ("DOTA") shall be:
      1. to obtain geospatial, hydrographic, meteorological, oceanographic and imagery intelligence about capabilities, intentions or activities of persons or organisations outside of Imperial Royal Union from the electromagnetic spectrum or other sources;
      2. to provide assistance and cooperate with public entities responsible for the production and use of geospatial, hydrographic, meteorological, oceanographic and imagery products and technologies; and
      3. to regulate the geospatial mapping of Imperial Royal Union by public and foreign entities.
  9. National Surveillance Institute
    1. The functions of the National Surveillance Institute ("NSI") shall be:
      1. to obtain intelligence about the capabilities, intentions and activities of persons or organisations in the form of electromagnetic, electrical, magnetic or acoustic energy;
      2. to prevent and disrupt, by electronic or similar means, cybercrime undertaken by persons or organisations outside of Imperial Royal Union;
      3. to prevent and disrupt, by electronic or similar means, foreign influenced or directed intelligence and espionage activities relating to electromagnetic, electrical, magnetic and acoustic energy mediums;
      4. to provide assistance to public entities in relation to cryptographic technologies and techniques; and
      5. to regulate lawful surveillance activities that may be taken against persons and organisations within Imperial Royal Union.
  10. State Intelligence Service
    1. The functions of the State Intelligence Service ("SIS") shall be:
      1. to obtain, in accordance with HIM Government's requirements, intelligence about the capabilities, intentions and activities of persons or organisations outside of Imperial Royal Union;
      2. to communicate such intelligence to and cooperate with other departments within HIM Government when necessary;
      3. to conduct misinformation and disinformation activities as well as covert operations for the furtherance of national interests in other nations; and
      4. to liaise with intelligence and security services, or other authorities, of other nations;
  11. State Security Support Service
    1. The functions of the State Security Support Service ("S4") shall be:
      1. to provide support and assistance to augment the capabilities of other State Security Organisations in foreign nations;
      2. to conduct periodical security clearance vetting and evaluation of employees of the Directorate-General;
      3. to vet, assess and evaluate new candidates for positions within the Directorate-General; and
      4. to investigate breaches and violations of protocols, procedures and directions by employees of the Directorate-General and report on the outcome of such investigations to the Director-General.
  12. Entity of interest
    1. The Director-General or the Director of any State Security Organisation may designate any person or organisation suspected to constitute a threat to national security as an entity of interest.
    2. No investigation or no operation shall be taken against such an entity until designation as an entity of interest.
    3. In designating the an entity as an entity of interest, the following factors shall be considered but shall not be taken to be exhaustive:
      1. probable cause for action, in particular existing interest by the Directorate-General of Public Security or foreign security agencies;
      2. records pertaining to the historical activities of the entity;
      3. affiliations and associations with past, present and potential entities of interest;
      4. actions and activities directly relating to the definition of a threat to national security; and
      5. complaints against the entity by members of the public.
  13. Dataset
    1. Intelligence gathered by the State Security Organisations shall be classed, grouped, compiled and consolidated into datasets. Datasets shall:
      1. contain all relevant information on the entity of interest, including analyses and interpretations of intelligence collected on such an entity;
      2. be secured with proper access control protocols, and include multiple layers of encryption and access authorisation where necessary; and
      3. maintain a log of all access and change records.
    2. Datasets shall be destroyed 10 years after the conclusion of any investigation into any entity of interest, unless:
      1. the investigation into such an entity has been declassified and records have been transferred onto the public domain; or
      2. the Director-General or the Director of the State Security Organisation responsible for the investigation has reason or probable cause to believe that the destruction of the dataset may impede any current ongoing investigation or be against the public interest.
    3. The Director-General may, at his own discretion or at the recommendation of a Director of any State Security Organisation, order the destruction of any dataset before the stipulated deadline for destruction, or before the conclusion of the investigation.
    4. Datasets may be admitted as evidence for prosecution by HIM Government, or as a defence by HIM Government in a court of law.
  14. Complaints and limitations to judicial oversight
    1. A complaint against the activities and operations of the Directorate-General in relation to exceeding its authority or scope of functions of any State Security Organisation shall be lodged to the Civil Service Procuratorate ("Procuratorate") within 7 days of the incident of relevance to the complaint.
    2. The Procuratorate shall assess the merits of the claims in the complaint and, with reference to the provisions of this Act in relation to the authority or scope of functions of the State Security Organisations, determine if further investigation is warranted. If further action is warranted, the Procuratorate shall inform the Director-General.
    3. The Director-General shall cooperate with the Procuratorate to investigate the complaint and report to the Procuratorate its findings and follow-up actions, if any, within 30 days of the complaint being lodged with the Procuratorate. If the Procuratorate determines the findings and follow-up actions to be unsatisfactory, it shall refer the investigation to the Supreme Court of Judicature.
    4. The Supreme Court of Judicature may exercise judicial oversight over the activities of the Directorate-General or the State Security Organisations only when referred to it by the Procuratorate. Any sitting Chief Justice may preside over the review in a closed session. The outcome of the review is final.
    5. The outcome or progress of any proceedings as a result of the complaint shall not be made public or made known to the complainant, unless declassified at a later date.
  15. Orders in general
    1. Any Order that is to be carried out within Imperial Royal Union shall only proceed under express written authorisation from the Director of the State Security Organisation responsible for the Order.
    2. Any Order shall satisfy the pre-requisite conditions that may be required for authorisation, and shall be revoked once such conditions are no longer satisfied or at the discretion of the Director as the Director may deem appropriate given the circumstances.
    3. Where an entity subjected to an Order has been taken into the custody of the Directorate-General of Public Security, under the jurisdiction of the Supreme Court of Judicature, or under the jusridiction of any military court or tribunal, the Order shall be automatically revoked.
    4. The Director-General or the Deputy Directors-General may revoke any Order issued by a Director.
  16. Special Detention Order
    1. A Special Detention Order may only be issued by the Director of DISC.
    2. A Special Detention Order and the conditions provided within shall be valid for a period of 1 week.
      1. The Director-General may extend a Special Detention Order for up to 6 months or revoke one that has not been extended before.
      2. Any sitting Chief Justice may extend a Special Detention Order for any period of time over 6 months and may also revoke or cancel any Special Detention Order in force.
    3. A Special Detention Order shall be issued or extended with respect to presence of probable cause, in which it shall satisfy the following criteria:
      1. the entity to which the Special Detention Order shall be imposed upon shall first be an entity of interest;
      2. there are sufficient grounds to believe that the entity constitutes a threat to national security, in which the material that such a belief may be based is current, with historical precedent, or a combination of both;
