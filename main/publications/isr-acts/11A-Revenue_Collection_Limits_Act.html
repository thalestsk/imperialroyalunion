ISR Cap. 11A
In Force

Revenue Collection Limits Act

  1. Short title and commencement
    1. This Act may be cited as the "Revenue Collection Limits Act".
    2. The following provisions of this Act are effective as of 24 April 2021.
  2. Collection of primary revenue
    1. The collection of primary revenue from the residents of Imperial Royal Union shall be conducted by the Cantonal Governments, in accordance with local written law in force as set forth by the Cantonal Legislatures, on behalf of the Public Revenue Administration, hereafter “Administration”.
  3. Limits on Revenue Order
    1. The total sum of revenue monies returned under a Revenue Order to the Administration shall not be less than 15% of the gross domestic product within a Canton or less than 40% of the total revenue collected by the Cantonal Government.
    2. The total sum of revenue monies returned under a Revenue Order to the Administration shall not be more than 60% of the total revenue collected by the Cantonal Government.
    3. Where the aforementioned limits may be in conflict, the lower limit shall take precedence.
    4. Where a shortfall may occur, the shortfall shall not be filled by public borrowing or the issuance of bonds, municipal or otherwise, on the part of the Cantonal Governments, and shall be instead filled by raising taxes or from alternative sources of revenue.
    5. Revenue derived from the investment returns or existing fiscal surpluses and reserves of the Cantonal Governments accumulated in the preceding fiscal periods shall not count towards the total revenue collected by the Cantonal Government, and neither shall it be used to fill a shortfall in the total sum of monies returned to the Public Revenue Administration.
  4. Enforcement of revenue collection
    1. For the purposes of this Act, any public service officer under the employment of the Cantonal Government and entrusted with the authority by the Cantonal Government to verify, confirm and approve tax filings, or to investigate and enforce against non-compliance shall be deemed a Revenue Collection Officer, hereafter, “officer”.
    2. Upon the filing of any tax notice, income statement, or any document which may be used for the certifying of tax payments, the accuracy and factualness of such a document shall first be verified by an officer, and the subsequent payment of such tax shall be verified by a second officer.
    3. Whereby a discrepancy, or the lack of accuracy or factualness, or where multiple instances of late tax filings, is or are discovered by an officer, the officer shall refer the tax filing to another officer who shall be responsible for the investigation of the discrepancy.
    4. An investigating officer, in the course of his investigation, shall send no more than 3 notices to the entity with a discrepancy regarding the entity’s tax filings, each spaced between 2 weeks to 4 weeks apart as a reminder to make the necessary corrections to the tax filing, and upon correction, shall arrange for an interview with the entity or its representatives regarding such corrections as deemed necessary by the investigating officer.
    5. Where the discrepancy exists on any tax filing in which the total value of tax to be paid exceeds a floating ceiling value set and revised annually by the Administration, or where no substantive correction or tax payment has been made after the third notice issued to the entity, or where the investigating officer is not satisfied with the outcome of the interview, if any, or the outcome of the investigation, the investigating officer shall refer and transfer full authority of the investigation to the Administration, which shall then be responsible for all subsequent enforcement actions.
    6. Where the investigating officer is satisfied with an entity’s tax filings upon serving at least 1 notice and where the necessary and accurate corrections have been made, but where the entity has been investigated for such discrepancies at least 2 other times, the investigating officer shall, under the advisement of a second officer, refer the investigation to the Administration, which shall then be responsible for all subsequent enforcement actions.
    7. The Administration may enforce against such instances via the imposition of fines as determined by the Comptroller-General of Public Revenue or refer ongoing investigations for prosecution and related criminal proceedings.
