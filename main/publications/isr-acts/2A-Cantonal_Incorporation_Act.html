ISR Cap. 2A
In Force

Cantonal Incorporation Act

  1. Short title and commencement
    1. This Act may be cited as the "INSERT_ACT_NAME_HERE".
    2. The following provisions of this Act are effective as of INSERT_DATE_HERE.
  2. Areal designations
    1. Within the geographical jurisdiction of each Canton, where the population of any named settlement is greater than 500,000 persons as recorded by the census of any 3 consecutive years of record within a period of 5 years, the area delimited by the boundaries of such a settlement shall be designated as an Incorporated Area.
    2. Within the geographical jurisdiction of each Canton, where the population of any named settlement does not exceed 500,000 persons as recorded by any census within the last 5 years, or where the settlement may yet to be officially named, the area delimited by the boundaries of such a settlement and its surrounding areas outside of such boundaries shall be designated as an Unincorporated Area.
    3. Where any named or unnamed settlement may not be automatically designated as an Incorporated Area or Unincorporated Area as defined by the aforementioned provisions of this Act, the Cantonal Government shall be responsible for the areal designation of such a settlement.
  3. Adjacent incorporated areas
    1. Metropolitan Areas shall be formed out of the agglomeration of a cluster of at least 10 adjacent Incorporated Areas with a combined population of no less than 30,000,000 persons. A Metropolitan Area may consist of as many Incorporated Areas that share a boundary with an Incorporated Area of the Metropolitan Area.
    2. Incorporated Areas that are non-adjacent to any Incorporated Area of a Metropolitan Area shall be agglomerated for the distribution and management of municipal services. Such agglomerated Incorporated Areas shall not exceed both the population or land area of the Cantonâ€™s Metropolitan Area, and where exceeded, the Cantonal Government shall split the agglomeration into 2 smaller agglomerations as is reasonably possible.
    3. Incorporated Areas, agglomerations of Incorporated Areas and Unincorporated Areas outside of the Metropolitan Area shall be arranged into Prefectures, at the discretion of the Cantonal Government.
  4. Additional elected representation
    1. Each Canton shall return to its respective Cantonal Legislature a fixed number of elected representatives based on the electoral system as may be defined by its respective Territorial Administrative Authority that shall be independent of the provisions of this Act.
    2. Each Metropolitan Area shall return to the respective Cantonal Legislature additional elected representatives of at least 30 but not more than 50, and shall be elected by the residents within the Metropolitan Area, according to the electoral system as may be defined by its respective Territorial Administrative Authority. The Cantonal Government shall designate 1 of the elected representatives as the Mayor of the Metropolitan Area.
    3. Each Prefecture shall return to the respective Cantonal Legislature additional elected representatives of at least 15 but not more than 25, and shall be elected by the residents within the Prefecture, according to the electoral system as may be defined by its respective Territorial Administrative Authority. The Cantonal Government shall designate 1 of the elected representatives as the Prefect of the Prefecture.
  5. Metropolitan administrations
    1. Each Cantonal Government shall establish a Metropolitan Administration for its respective Metropolitan Area, headed by the Mayor. The Metropolitan Administration shall be responsible for the coordination and provision of municipal services within the Metropolitan Area, and any other responsibilities as may be delegated or defined by the Cantonal Government or Cantonal Legislature.
  6. Prefectural authorities
    1. Each Cantonal Government shall establish a Prefectural Authority for each Prefecture within the Canton, headed by the Prefect. A Prefectural Authority shall be responsible for the coordination and provision of municipal services within the Prefecture as may be defined by the Cantonal Government or Cantonal Legislature, and shall not undertake responsibilities outside of the scope of municipal services. Prefectural Authorities shall be directly accountable of the Cantonal Government or a designated office within the Cantonal Government.
  7. Appointment of Mayors and Prefects
    1. The Governor-General, with or without the advice of any office within the Cantonal Government, is responsible for the appointment of the Mayor.
    2. The Governor-General, at the advice of the appropriate office within the Cantonal Government, is responsible for the appointment of Prefects.
    3. The Cantonal Legislature shall not impose any form of restraint regarding the power of the Governor-General in appointing the Mayor or Prefects.
    4. Mayors and Prefects may vacate their office and in such an event shall also vacate their seat within the Cantonal Legislature, through which such a vacancy shall be effected by a combination of any 2 of the following events or by expiration of the term of office:
      1. a decision of the Governor-General;
      2. a majority vote of no-confidence by the Cantonal Legislature with majority; or
      3. a two-thirds majority vote of no-confidence by the additional elected representatives of the Metropolitan Area or respective Prefecture with two-thirds majority.
    5. Where a vacancy arises, the Governor-General shall appoint a new Mayor or Prefect within 10 days of the vacancy. The outgoing Mayor or Prefect may designate an Acting Mayor or Acting Prefect in the interim until the appointment of a new Mayor or Prefect by the Governor-General.
    6. The Governor-General may delay the appointment of a new Mayor or Prefect should an election arising due to the expiration of term of office or for the purpose of filling a vacant seat be stipulated to be held within 100 days of the vacancy.
  8. Remuneration
    1. Mayors and Prefects shall be remunerated at a rate determined by their respective Cantonal Government or Cantonal Legislature and shall be at least twice the amount remunerated to an elected representative in the Cantonal Legislature.
