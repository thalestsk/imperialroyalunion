ISR Cap. 11
In Force

Inland Revenue Act

  1. Short title and commencement
    1. This Act may be cited as the "Inland Revenue Act".
    2. The following provisions of this Act are effective as of 24 April 2021.
  2. Public Revenue Administration
    1. The Public Revenue Administration ("Administration”) shall be the body within HIM Government responsible for the collection and consolidation of revenue.
  3. Comptroller-General of Public Revenue
    1. The Comptroller-General of Public Revenue (“Comptroller-General”) shall be the head of the Administration.
    2. The Comptroller-General shall be appointed by the Emperor.
    3. The Comptroller-General shall be responsible for:
      1. the collection of revenue monies for HIM Government;
      2. the redistribution and disbursement of collected revenue monies to Cantonal Governments and the Territorial Administrative Authorities;
      3. the disbursement of revenue monies for the purposes of financing the operational requirements and the projects and schemes undertaken by HIM Government;
      4. the disbursement of revenue monies for the remuneration of public service officers within the direct employment of HIM Government; and
      5. the allocation of revenue monies for investment purposes.
    4. The Comptroller-General may appoint subordinate officers to exercise his responsibilities on his behalf, and deputise for him where and when appropriate.
  4. Revenue and Appropriation Orders
    1. The collection of revenue monies from the application of taxes on the residents of Imperial Royal Union shall be regarded as the primary revenue stream and shall be collected from the Cantonal Governments via the issuance of Revenue Orders.
    2. The collection of revenue monies from import tariffs, levies and duties shall be conducted via the issuance of Revenue Orders that shall be enforced by the Customs & Excise Agency, which shall be regarded as part of the secondary revenue stream.
    3. Revenue monies derived from investment returns, administrative fees to HIM Government, and penalty fines as a result of violations and criminal offences shall be regarded as part of the secondary revenue stream.
    4. The disbursement, distribution, allocation, transfer, and appropriation of revenue monies collected by the Administration shall be made through Appropriation Orders.
    5. The Comptroller-General shall only issue Revenue Orders and Appropriation Orders when
      1. under the instruction of the Emperor, or
      2. under the legislative authority of the Senate,
    6. in which the Revenue Orders and Appropriation Orders shall only be valid for the fiscal period in which they have been issued during.
  5. Additional powers
    1. The Comptroller-General may exercise additional powers for the purposes of enforcement against the fraudulent or negligent filing of tax notices, and in instances of tax evasion, where the Comptroller-General shall be empowered to:
      1. conduct investigations and inquiries into instances of fraud or evasion;
      2. recommend criminal proceedings against individuals found to have committed instances of fraud and evasion for prosecution; and
      3. seize assets and impose fines on such individuals to cover for the shortfall in revenue which would have been originally collected but for the instances of fraud and evasion;
    2. The additional powers of the Comptroller-General shall be exercised at the referral of the Cantonal Governments, or any other revenue-collecting body authorised under this Act, upon the discovery of instances of fraud and evasion to which such revenue-collecting body transfers the authority to enforce local laws in force regarding revenue collection to the Comptroller-General.
