ISR Cap. 8
In Force

Civil Service (Governance) Act

  1. Short title and commencement
    1. This Act may be cited as the "Civil Service (Governance) Act".
    2. The following provisions of this Act are effective as of 7 November 2021.
  2. Central authority
    1. This Act establishes the Public Service Secretariat ("Secretariat") as the central executive body responsible for the discharge of responsibilities and delivery of services in the public sector, and the departments of the Council of Supervision ("Council") as the oversight authority on the Secretariat.
  3. Public service officer
    1. A public service officer ("officer") shall be authorised with the powers to act on behalf of the Emperor or HIM Government that may be necessary for the discharge of his duties as an officer.
    2. Employees of HIM Government with a direct contract of service between the employee and any entity or body of HIM Government shall be deemed as officers.
    3. Employees of non-governmental entities or bodies that have contracts for the provision of services to HIM Government shall not be deemed as officers.
    4. The ranks of officers in the Secretariat shall follow the order of precedence specified in the First Schedule of this Act.
  4. References to departments
    1. For the purpose of this Act and any reference to this Act, all references to "departments" and "departmental heads" shall refer to a department subordinated to the Secretariat and its highest-ranking officer in the senior leadership or management of the department respectively.
    2. The types of departments under the Secretariat and their respective departmental heads are as follows:
      1. Government Commissions, headed by a Chairman;
      2. Executive Support Offices, headed by a Permanent Secretary;
      3. Statutory Departments, headed by a Director; and
      4. Affiliated Public Bodies, headed by a Chief Executive.
    3. There shall be no variation in the titles for departmental heads unless otherwise specified in any other written law in force, specifically within the constitutional act for the department.
    4. Departmental heads shall hold the rank of "Director" unless otherwise specified in any other written law in force, with the exception of departmental heads pursuant to paragraph (2) (b) of this section, who shall at least be of rank "Assistant Associate Permanent Secretary".
    5. Any reference to the departmental head as the representative of the department, the department itself, the civil service or the Public Service Secretariat shall be considered to be interchangeable.
  5. Chief Permanent Secretary
    1. The Chief Permanent Secretary shall be the highest-ranking officer within Secretariat. He shall have the powers to:
      1. appoint and dismiss officers of and above the rank of "Assistant Associate Permanent Secretary", subject to the confirmation of the Emperor and any other written law in force regarding the appointment of such officers to any given position;
      2. appoint and dismiss departmental heads, subject to any other written law in force, specifically the constitutional acts of departments relating to the appointment of departmental heads;
      3. give directions as to the performance by the public body of its functions;
      4. enact and implement Directives as is necessary for the efficacious discharge of public service;
      5. authorise officers to exercise the powers of the Chief Permanent Secretary; and
      6. exercise any other executive power as may be granted at the discretion of the Emperor.
  6. Independent advisors
    1. Departmental heads may appoint independent advisors from outside of the public sector to advise on certain matters as may be necessary.
    2. Independent advisors shall not be considered as officers.
    3. Independent advisers shall not have any decision-making authority within the department.
  7. Functions, duties and powers of departments
    1. The functions, duties, and powers of a new or an existing department may be determined or altered by the Chief Permanent Secretary, except as provided in this Act or any other written law in force.
    2. The Chief Permanent Secretary may decide that a working arrangement will apply only if they are reasonably satisfied that this is necessary or desirable for the department to perform its functions or will no longer apply only if they are reasonably satisfied that this is no longer the case.
  8. General responsibilities of departmental heads
    1. A departmental head is responsible to the Emperor for:
      1. improving ways of working across departments and officers at different levels of government;
      2. their departmentsâ€™s responsiveness on matters relating to the collective interests of government;
      3. the operation of their department, including in carrying out the purpose of the public service;
      4. providing advice on the long-term implications of policies;
      5. maintaining public institutions, assets, and liabilities;
      6. the integrity and conduct of the employees for whom the departmental head is responsible; and
      7. the efficient and economical delivery of the goods or services provided by the department and how effectively those goods or services contribute to the intended outcomes.
  9. Promotions on merit
    1. When promoting an officer to an appointment of higher position or rank under this Act, preference must be given to the person who is best suited to the position.
  10. Selections for appointments to the civil service
    1. This section applies to the selection of persons who are not officers for appointment to the civil service.
    2. A person's selection must be on merit on the basis of fair and open competition.
    3. A selection may be excepted from the requirement of paragraph (2) of this section if the selection was made based on a set of published guidelines relating to the recruitment of new officers by the Chief Permanent Secretary.
  11. Decision-making by departments
    1. The senior leadership or management of a department must regulate their own procedure in accordance with this Act and any other specific requirements prescribed by any other written law in force, specifically the constitutional act of the department.
    2. Decisions undertaken by the senior leadership or management must be recorded in the minutes of meetings.
  12. Annual and supplementary estimates
    1. A department must, in every financial year of the department, prepare or cause to be prepared, and must adopt a statement containing annual estimates of its revenue and expenditure for the following financial year.
    2. A department may, during its financial year, prepare or cause to be prepared, and may adopt, a statement containing supplementary estimates of its revenue and expenditure for that financial year.
  13. Financial accounts and records
    1. A department must:
      1. keep proper accounts and records of its transactions and affairs and in accordance with the requirements of written law in force;
      2. ensure adequate control is maintained over the property and assets of, or in the custody of, the department and over the expenditure incurred by the department; and
      3. ensure all payments out of its monies are correctly made and properly authorised.
  14. Authority to share data
    1. A department is authorised to share the information under its control with another department to the extent permitted by the any direction on the sharing of data regardless of any obligation as to confidentiality under the common law.
    2. The provisions of paragraph (1) does not override any obligation as to confidentiality because of legal privilege or contract.
