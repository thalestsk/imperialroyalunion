ISR Cap. 5
In Force

Armed Forces Act

  1. Short title and commencement
    1. This Act may be cited as the "Armed Forces Act".
    2. The following provisions of this Act are effective as of 11 April 2021.
  2. Chain of command and direct authority
    1. The Emperor of Imperial Royal Union is the commander-in-chief of the armed forces, which shall comprise of, non-exhaustively, of all military and paramilitary forces and any other forces therewithin established with an object of carrying out combat or peacekeeping duties.
    2. The Council of War ("Council") shall, under the direct authority and instruction of the Emperor, assist and advice the Emperor in the command and control of the armed forces and the organisations and forces that may be assigned into the purview of the Council.
    3. The Council shall be chaired by the Emperor, and in the absence of the Emperor, chaired by a representative of the Emperor appointed at the personal discretion of the Emperor.
    4. The appointment of members of the Council shall be at the personal discretion of the Emperor.
  3. Separation of authority
    1. To ensure the efficacy of the command-and-control structure and to provide for utmost accountability to the Emperor, the Council shall separate the administrative authority from the operational powers, thereby establishing the National Defence Administration and the Imperialean National Armed Forces as the civilian-managed administrative authority for defence matters and the umbrella organisation for the standing and operational military and paramilitary forces of Imperial Royal Union respectively.
  4. Armed Forces Code
    1. The Armed Forces Code, (“Code”) shall be the law in which all persons serving in the Imperialean National Armed Forces shall be additionally bound to on top of any existing laws in force.
    2. The Code shall be drafted, maintained and amended by the Council.
    3. The Council may issue General Orders which shall be treated to be part of the Code.
    4. Within the Imperialean National Armed Forces, the respective chiefs of the services may issue Standing Orders, without being contrary to existing General Orders in force, which shall be treated as complementary and supplementary to General Orders and to be a part of the Code.
    5. Directives issued within the Imperialean National Armed Forces for the purpose of the provision of additional guidance for internal reference shall not be treated as a part of the Code.
  5. Liability and jurisdiction
    1. On whom the Code is applicable, such a person shall be liable to be tried in a military court or disciplinary officer for military offences committed whilst in active service.
    2. Nothing in this Act shall affect the jurisdiction of civil courts to try a person subject to the Code for any civil offence.
  6. Director-General of Defence Administration
    1. The National Defence Administration shall be headed by the Director-General of Defence Administration (“Director-General”).
    2. The Director-General may appoint any number of subordinate officers to which he may delegate to such officers the authority to discharge the powers and duties of the Director-General, and revoke such authority in due course, at the discretion of the Director-General.
    3. The powers that may be exercised the Director-General shall include:
      1. the general administration of the National Defence Administration comprising of the management of the organisation’s internal processes and systems;
      2. the formulation and implementation of a coherent and contemporary defence policy;
      3. the contracting, design, experimentation, and procurement of armaments and technological innovations for the Imperialean National Armed Forces;
      4. the planning and preparation of material, inclusive of stockpiling and management of reserves, for an outbreak of war;
      5. the formulation of the schedule in which the ranks, grades and corresponding remuneration of military officers and persons bound by the Code are defined;
      6. the management of the enlistment process and the national service policy;
      7. the development of the defence budget; and
      8. any other powers as may be conferred to the Director-General within the overarching scope of work relating to matters of defence.
  7. Imperialean National Armed Forces
    1. The Imperialean National Armed Forces shall comprise of the following service branches:
      1. the Universal Defence Command, with the Joint Chief of Defence Staff as the service chief, responsible for the command of combat units in active deployment in joint theatres and coordination of joint response;
      2. the Paramilitary Reserves Command, with the Chief of Paramilitary Forces as the service chief, responsible for the command of paramilitary and combat support units deployed in non-combat or post-combat zones;
      3. the Grand Imperial Navy, with the Chief of Naval Staff as the service chief, responsible for the command and development of standing naval forces not in active deployment and naval reserves to augment joint responses; and
      4. the Grand Imperial Army, with the Chief of General Staff as the service chief, responsible for the command and development of standing army forces not in active deployment and army reserves to augment joint responses.
  8. Chief of Combined Armed Forces
    1. The Chief of Combined Armed Forces shall be the chief coordinating military officer of the Imperialean National Armed Forces and the chief military advisor to the Emperor.
    2. The Chief of Combined Armed Forces shall be appointed at the discretion of the Emperor and shall have served as a service chief for no less than 2 years.
    3. The Chief of Combined Armed Forces shall be responsible for:
      1. coordinating and overseeing military operations between the service branches;
      2. planning for the succession of military leadership;
      3. maintaining the alertness and readiness of the Imperialean National Armed Forces; and
      4. other responsibilities which may be assigned by the Emperor.
  9. Service Chiefs
    1. Service chiefs shall only exercise their authority within their respective service branches, unless explicitly authorised by the Chief of Combined Armed Forces and with notice to the Director-General to do otherwise.
    2. Service chiefs shall be appointed by the Chief of Combined Armed Forces and shall have served as a senior officer within their respective service branch for no less than 10 years in both operational and management positions.
    3. The Chief of Combined Armed Forces shall also appoint immediate deputies to service chiefs, who shall serve as the second-in-command within the service branch and shall assume the responsibilities as the acting service chief when the service chief is unavailable.
    4. Within their respective service branches, service chiefs shall:
      1. exercise command and control authority over the units of the service branch;
      2. enforce discipline and uphold the morale of their standing forces;
      3. report to the Chief of Combined Armed Forces on the state of operational readiness of their service branch and implement appropriate measures to ensure a satisfactory state;
      4. appoint subordinate officers, with the exception of their immediate deputy, to assist in their command and management responsibilities; and
      5. other responsibilities as may be required by the Chief of Combined Armed Forces.
  10. Chief of War Staff
    1. The Chief of War Staff shall be the head of the Krigsmaskin, a shared department within the National Defence Administration and Imperialean National Armed Forces, and be responsible for:
      1. the preparation of the Imperialean economy for a transition to a wartime economy;
      2. the transformation of the Imperialean economy into a wartime economy prior to or at the outbreak of hostilities with or without a declaration of war; and
      3. the steering of the war effort and wartime economy.
    2. The Chief of War Staff shall be nominated by both the Chief of Combined Armed Forces and Director-General and then formally appointed by the Emperor.
