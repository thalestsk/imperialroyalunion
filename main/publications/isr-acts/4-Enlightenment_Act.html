ISR Cap. 4
In Force

Enlightenment Act

  1. Short title and commencement
    1. This Act may be cited as the "Enlightenment Act".
    2. The following provisions of this Act are effective as of 11 April 2021.
  2. Public atheism
    1. The practise of, participation in, undertaking and profession of religious activities, pagan superstitions, animistic or otherwise, otherworldly beliefs and faith-related spiritual commitments, of which the extent of such unenlightened practices is non-exhaustive and shall be representative of practices within the general category of religious or faith-related activities, shall be prohibited, without exception.
  3. Offences relating to Penal Code
    1. For an offence committed under this Act, whether by omission or not, intentionally or negligently, the offender shall be automatically guilty beyond reasonable doubt of an offence relating to public nuisance and shall bear in full the penalties of such an offence as may be determined by the Penal Code (ISR Cap. 99), in addition to the individual penalties of each offence defined within this Act.
  4. Penalties for conscionable illiteracy
    1. An attempt to commit an offence, or to abet such an offence, or to attempt to abet such an offence, shall constitute the commission of the offence in itself.
    2. Whoever displays a religious symbol or a fashion accessory or clothing with religious affiliations, in public or otherwise, with or without prior warning for the removal of such a display, shall be punished with imprisonment for a term of 1 years and with a fine of IRE 5,000.
    3. Whoever engages in the practise of religion in opposition to public atheism shall be punished with imprisonment for a term of 2 years and with a fine of IRE 7,000.
    4. Whoever publicly professes any form of religious beliefs, for as long as such profession may be minimally discernible to any member of the public shall be punished with imprisonment for a term of 3 years and with a fine of IRE 10,000.
    5. Whoever engages in the operation of any place of worship for the purpose of practice of religion, or for the propagation and proselytization of such religious beliefs, regardless of concealment or limitations to access from the public, shall be punished with imprisonment for a term of 4 years and with a fine of IRE 15,000.
    6. Whoever provides as justification, official or unofficial, be it written or verbal, their religious beliefs, in order to dissuade, discourage or prevent the lawful discharge of public duty to the State or private contractual duty, shall be punished with imprisonment for a term of 5 years.
  5. Academic studies
    1. Any form of academic study and the import or export of material in relation to such study on religion, faith-centric cultures, mythology and folklore shall be regulated by the Institute of Cultural Education, in which the Director of Cultural Education shall have the authority to approve such a study and its associated activities, to which the Director shall file a report with sufficient justification for the grant of approval for such a study within 24 hours of the grant of approval. Where an offence is unintentional and arises out of the purpose of academic study, and where all reasonable steps have been taken to minimise the commission of such an offence, no offence shall be deemed to have been committed.
  6. Properties relating to Places of Worship
    1. Where a property is discovered to have allowed for the operation of a place of worship, by omission or commission, the property shall be forfeited to HIM Government with immediate effect.
  7. Religious artifacts
    1. Where a religious artifact may be uncovered, discovered, manufactured, or reproduced, the artifact shall be sold or auctioned off in a foreign nation, with the proceeds of such sale or auction returned to the HIM Government, even if the artifact might not have under the original custody of HIM Government.
