ISR Cap. 32
In Force

Public Security (Conduct) Act

  1. Short title and commencement
    1. This Act may be cited as the "Public Security (Conduct) Act".
    2. The following provisions of this Act are effective as of 26 April 2021.
  2. Professional standards
    1. Within the Directorate-General of Public Security, hereafter “Directorate-General”, the Deputy Director-General for Administration shall be responsible for the office within the Directorate-General for the upholding of professional standards, which shall comprise of both an internal investigatory unit and an independent reviewing unit to ensure the integrity of any professional standards work.
    2. The Standards of Professional Behaviour established shall be the standards of professional behaviour as may be determined by the Deputy Director-General for Administration, to which all public security officers (“officers”) shall be expected to follow.
  3. Disciplinary hearing
    1. A disciplinary hearing shall be the final binding authority regarding the outcome of disciplinary proceedings and actions, if any, that are to be taken against an officer.
    2. The disciplinary hearing shall comprise of at least 4 reviewing officers, namely the appropriate authority being the direct superior of the officer concerned, a senior officer from the office responsible for professional standards, the investigating officer, and one other independent officer with no direct affiliation within the past 10 years to the officer concerned.
    3. A disciplinary hearing shall be preceded by a formal investigation into the actions of the officer concerned, in which the officer concerned shall have been present in at least 1 interview on the record with the investigating officer, and the material, evidence and information obtained in the course of the investigation shall be handed over to the disciplinary hearing.
    4. Where the precluding investigation is unable to present substantive evidence against the conduct or actions of the officer concerned, the investigating officer, under the advisement of his appropriate authority, shall conclude the investigation without referring the investigation for a disciplinary hearing and shall also provide in writing the justifications for concluding the investigation.
    5. Any disciplinary hearing shall be conducted within a month of the referral of an investigation to the hearing, at a date determined by the most senior officer in the hearing.
    6. Where the investigating officer is not an officer from within the Directorate-General, a separate disciplinary hearing may be conducted in place of or simultaneously with any hearing carried out within the Directorate-General, and in the latter instance, the hearing which produces a stricter outcome shall be the final binding authority, unless otherwise specified by other written law.
  4. Investigating officer
    1. The appointed investigating officer for the investigation into actions and conduct of the officer concerned shall be from a different unit from that of the officer concerned and shall be at least 1 rank higher than the officer concerned, although he may be assisted throughout the course of the investigation by officers of lower rank, and that the appropriate authority shall not be granted any investigatory authority over the officer concerned.
    2. Where the officer concerned is of rank “Assistant Superintendent” and higher but lower than the rank of “Deputy Assistant Commissioner” as described in the First Schedule of the Public Security (Organisation) Act (ISR Cap. 30), the investigating officer shall be at least 2 ranks higher than the officer concerned.
    3. Where the officer concerned is of rank “Deputy Assistant Commissioner” and higher as described in the First Schedule of the Public Security (Organisation) Act (ISR Cap. 30), the investigating officer shall be from an independent body outside of the Directorate-General.
  5. Legal representation
    1. Subject to paragraph (2) of this section, the officer concerned has the right to be legally represented, by a preferred legal professional of his choice at any interview during the course of the investigation and the subsequent disciplinary hearing, if any.
    2. The unavailability of one or more preferred legal professionals is not a valid ground for delaying an interview or disciplinary hearing where an alternative legal professional can be found.
    3. The officer concerned may revoke his rights to representation voluntarily, or involuntarily where specified in accordance with other written law.
  6. Provision of notices and documents
    1. Where any written notice or document is to be given or supplied to the officer concerned under this Act, it must be:
      1. given to the officer in person;
      2. left with a person at, or sent by recorded delivery to, the officer’s last known address; or
      3. given to the officer in any other manner agreed between the officer and the person who is required to give the notice or document and the officer.
    2. Where notice is served requesting the compliance of the officer concerned with the instructions contained within such a notice, the officer concerned shall be required to inform his appropriate authority of such a notice being served to him.
  7. Suspension
    1. The appropriate authority, under the advisement of the investigating officer may, subject to the provisions of this section, suspend the officer concerned from his duties and his rights and access to the Directorate-General, in which the officer concerned shall surrender his warrant of authority.
    2. An officer who is suspended under this section remains a public security officer and shall not have his salary and other remunerable benefits withheld from him for the duration of the suspension.
    3. The appropriate authority may not suspend a police officer under this section unless the following conditions are satisfied:
      1. having considered temporary redeployment to alternative duties or an alternative location as an alternative to suspension, the appropriate authority has determined that such redeployment is not appropriate in circumstances possible at the time of suspension; and
      2. it appears to the appropriate authority that the effective investigation of the case may be prejudiced unless the officer concerned is so suspended; or
      3. it is known to the appropriate authority that having sufficient regard to the nature of the allegation and any other relevant considerations, the public interest requires that the officer should be so suspended.
    4. The appropriate authority may suspend the officer concerned with effect from the date and time of notice which must be given in writing with a summary of the reasons.
    5. The officer concerned or his legal representative may make representations against suspension to the appropriate authority:
      1. before the end of 7 working days beginning with the first working day after being suspended; or
      2. at any time during the suspension if the officer concerned reasonably believes that circumstances relevant to the conditions for suspension have changed.
    6. The appropriate authority must review the conditions for suspension:
      1. upon receipt of any representations made under paragraph (5) of this section;
      2. if there has been no previous review, before the end of 4 weeks beginning with the first working day after the suspension; or
      3. in any other case when he becomes aware that the circumstances relevant to the conditions for suspension may have changed.
    7. Where, following a review under paragraph (6) of this section, the suspension conditions remain satisfied and the appropriate authority decides the suspension should continue, it must, before the end of 3 working days beginning with the day after the review, so notify the officer concerned in writing with a summary of the reasons.
    8. Where the officer concerned is suspended under this regulation, the officer must remain so suspended until whichever of the following occurs first:
      1. the appropriate authority decides, following a review, that the conditions for suspension are no longer satisfied;
      2. the outcome of the investigation or its subsequent disciplinary hearing affirms and clears the conduct and actions of the officer concerned; or
      3. the officer concerned resigns from his duties as a public security officer, whether voluntarily or involuntarily arising from the outcome of any disciplinary hearings.
  8. Record of disciplinary proceedings
    1. The appropriate authority must cause a record to be kept of disciplinary hearings brought against every officer concerned, together with the finding and decision on disciplinary action and the decision in any appeal by the officer.
  9. Preliminary to investigation
    1. The appropriate authority must assess whether the conduct, which is the subject matter of the allegation, would directly or indirectly implicate the Directorate-General in relation to controversial, questionable, malfeasant or discreditable conduct, the appropriate authority must assess whether:
      1. the conduct, if proved, would amount to practice requiring improvement;
      2. the conduct, if proved, would amount misconduct;
      3. the implications for the Directorate-General are wide-ranging and limitless; or
      4. no further action is warranted.
    2. In the conduct of such an assessment under paragraph (1) (b) of this section by the appropriate authority, the appropriate authority shall consult with his superior officers and seek clarifications from the officer concerned where necessary.
    3. In the conduct of such an assessment under paragraph (1) (c) of this section by the appropriate authority, the appropriate authority shall consult with his superior officers and the appointed legal representative from within the Directorate-General.
    4. Where the assessment finds the allegations to fall under paragraph (1) (a) of this section, the appropriate authority shall take the necessary steps to ensure improvement in future practice.
    5. Where the assessment finds the allegations to fall under paragraph (1) (d) of this section, the appropriate authority shall notify the officer concerned in writing as soon as practicable.
    6. Where the assessment is in accordance with paragraphs (2) and (3) of this section in which such an assessment is reached, the appropriate authority shall refer the allegations to an investigation in which the investigating officer shall the officer concerned in writing within 24 hours of the referral.
  10. Appointment of investigating officer
    1. The referral to investigation shall be lodged to an authorised professional standards unit within the Directorate-General, and the most senior officer of such a unit, in the appointment of an investigating officer, must be satisfied that any investigating officer appointed by him:
      1. is qualified to plan and manage the investigation;
      2. is not an interested party and no conflict of interest is likely to exist; and
      3. has not served with or under the management of the officer concerned in the past 7 years.
  11. Objective of investigation
    1. The purpose of the investigation is to:
      1. gather evidence to establish the facts and circumstances of the alleged misconduct, and
      2. determine and establish the grounds, if any, for a disciplinary hearing.
    2. The investigating officer must as soon as practicable after being appointed draw up the terms of reference of the investigation.
    3. Where the investigation is not conclusive within 12 months from the date at which the allegation was made known to the appropriate authority of the officer concerned, the investigating officer close the investigation and make the relevant decisions about the investigation as it is unless a written approval is obtained from his immediate superior for an extension of 6 months.
  12. Representations to investigating officer
    1. Upon receipt of notice pertaining to the investigation, the officer concerned may provide, within a submission period, a written or oral statement relating to any matter under investigation to the investigating officer, including any mitigating circumstances relevant to any such matter, and support such a statement by providing any relevant documents to the investigating officer.
    2. The investigating officer must, as part of the investigation, consider any such statement or document and must make a record of having received it.
    3. The submission period shall be the immediate 10 working days upon the serving of notice to the officer concerned, unless otherwise specified within the notice or extended by the investigating officer in which the investigating officer shall put on record the reasons for extension.
  13. Interviews during investigation
    1. No interview may take place until the officer concerned has been provided with the terms of reference and served with a notice of the investigation.
    2. The investigating officer shall supply to the officer concerned written notice of the date, time, and place of the interview.
    3. The investigating officer must, in advance of the interview, provide the officer concerned with such information as the investigating officer considers appropriate in the circumstances of the case to enable the officer to prepare for the interview.
    4. Interviews may be conducted with other officers and persons of interest who may bear witness of or provide further information regarding the allegation at the subject of investigation or any other actions which are to be addressed by the terms of reference.
    5. The representations and evidence gathered from any interview shall not be admissible if no video and audio record of the interview exists.
  14. Report on investigation
    1. On completion of the investigation, the investigating officer must as soon as practicable submit a written report on the investigation to his immediate superior.
    2. The written report must:
      1. provide an accurate summary of the evidence;
      2. attach or refer to any relevant documents; and
      3. indicate the investigating officer’s opinion, relevant conclusions and all supporting justifications, inductions, and deductions as to whether there is a case to answer in respect of misconduct or whether there is no case to answer.
    3. Where there is a case to answer in respect of misconduct, the matter shall be referred to a disciplinary hearing.
    4. Where there is no case to answer, the investigation shall be closed and a copy of the report shall be sent to appropriate authority of the officer concerned along with the recommended actions to be taken, if any, and a written notice served to the officer concerned regarding the conclusion of the investigation.
    5. The investigating officer may reopen the investigation in light of the belief on the part of the investigating officer of a change in circumstances or the discovery of new evidence or information.
  15. Withdrawal of disciplinary hearing
    1. At any time before the beginning or during the conduct of the disciplinary hearing, the members of the hearing if it is no longer satisfied that there is a case to answer in respect of misconduct, must direct that the case be withdrawn and the disciplinary hearings to cease, provided that at least 1 other officer in the hearing who is not the officer concerned is in accord with both the most senior officer on the hearing and the investigating officer.
  16. Involvement of third parties
    1. Third parties such as the complainant and other interested parties shall not be in attendance of the disciplinary hearing, and the disclosure of the outcome of such hearing or the investigation shall be at the discretion of the Deputy Director-General for Administration.
  17. Outcome of proceedings
    1. Disciplinary action may be imposed if at least 3 members on the hearing agree to the exact action to be imposed.
    2. Where the conduct is proved and amounts to misconduct, the disciplinary action available is:
      1. a written warning, in which no promotion or salary increase shall be effected within 2 years of receipt of such a warning;
      2. a final written warning, in which any subsequent disciplinary hearing arising from future conduct would immediately amount to dismissal without notice;
      3. a reduction in rank and salary;
      4. dismissal without notice, in which the officer concerned shall cease to be a beneficiary of the benefits associated with resignation with notice or retirement; or
      5. administrative leave without salary and benefits for up to 1 year.
    3. Disciplinary action must be in regard of the record of the officer concerned as shown on the officer’s personal record, taking into account testimonies in support for the officer concerned, if any.
    4. The officer concerned shall be served with a written notice on the outcome of the disciplinary hearing within 10 days of the hearing, and the grounds for any disciplinary action, if any.
