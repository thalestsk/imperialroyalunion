ISR Cap. 6
In Force

Currency and Reserve Bank Act

  1. Short title and commencement
    1. This Act may be cited as the "Currency and Reserve Bank Act".
    2. The following provisions of this Act are effective as of 15 May 2021.
  2. Central Monetary Authority
    1. The central and reserve bank of Imperial Royal Union shall be the Central Monetary Authority (“Authority”) and shall have the authority over matters connected therewith.
    2. The Authority shall be led by the Board of Governors (“Board”), presided over by a Chairman appointed at the discretion of the Emperor, with each Canton appointing to the Board a Governor who shall be the incumbent head of their cantonal reserve banking system subordinate to the Authority but operating within the written laws in force of their local Canton.
    3. The Emperor may appoint any number of Special Advisors to the Board.

  3. General powers of the Authority
    1. The Authority, through the Board, shall have the sole right to issue coins and notes in Imperial Royal Union, with the exception of the Overseas Territories.
    2. The Board shall have the right to oversee and issue directions to the local reserve banking systems of the Overseas Territories but may not issue currency on their behalf.
    3. In line with the requirements and conditions as may be periodically set forth by the Emperor or any representative acting on the behalf of the Emperor, the Board shall determine the monetary policy of Imperial Royal Union. In doing so, the Board shall also adhere to the following objectives, unless otherwise stated in a written order by the Emperor, which are the objectives of:
      1. achieving and maintaining stability in the general level of prices over the medium term;
      2. supporting maximum sustainable employment; and
      3. protecting the efficiency and soundness of the financial system.
    4. The Board shall appoint the Commissioner of Currency (“Commissioner”), who shall be responsible for overseeing and managing the administrative and operational requirements of the Authority in daily operations.
    5. With regards to the issuance of currency and matters connected therewith, with the exception of monetary policy operations, any reference to the Commissioner and Authority shall be construed as interchangeable and mutually non-exclusive.
    6. The Commissioner may appoint any number of subordinate officers and delegate his responsibilities, duties and powers accordingly, and may revoke such powers as he may deem necessary, subject to the provisions of other written laws in force.
  4. Currency of Imperial Royal Union
    1. With regards to the issuance of currency and matters connected therewith, with the exception of monetary policy operations, any reference to the Commissioner and Authority shall be construed as interchangeable and mutually non-exclusive.
    2. The Commissioner may appoint any number of subordinate officers and delegate his responsibilities, duties and powers accordingly, and may revoke such powers as he may deem necessary, subject to the provisions of other written laws in force.
  5. Use of Imperial Royale Elite
    1. All monetary obligations or transactions in Imperial Royal Union shall be deemed to be expressed and recorded and shall be settled in the Imperial Royale Elite unless otherwise provided for by law or validly agreed upon between the parties.
  6. Right to legal tender
    1. Currency notes issued by the Commissioner, if the notes have not been illegally dealt with, shall be legal tender up to their face value for the payment of any amount.
    2. Coins of a denomination issued by the Authority, if the coins have not been illegally dealt with, are legal tender up to their face value for the payment of an amount not exceeding 20 times the face value of a coin of that denomination.
    3. Where a payee has given written notice to the payer that he would not accept as payment all or any of the denominations of currency notes or coins in satisfaction of a debt, paragraphs (1) or (2) of this section, as the case may be, shall not apply to the payment of the debt to the extent specified in the notice.
    4. For the purposes of this Act:
      1. a coin shall be deemed to have been illegally dealt with where the coin has been impaired, diminished, or lightened otherwise than by fair wear and tear, or has been defaced by having any name, word, device, or number stamped or engraved thereon, whether the coin has or has not been thereby diminished or lightened; and
      2. a currency note shall be deemed to have been illegally dealt with where the note has been impaired, diminished or affected otherwise than by fair wear and tear, or has been defaced by writing or impressing on any note any mark, word, letter, or figure or by perforation, cutting, splitting or in any other manner, whether the note has or has not been thereby impaired or diminished.
    5. In any criminal proceedings in which the genuineness of any currency note or coin may be in question, a certificate signed by an officer of the Authority authorised for that purpose that he is satisfied by personal examination that such note or coin is or is not forged shall be held to be conclusive evidence of the same.
    6. The Authority is not liable to guarantee the face value or physical value of any currency note or coin not issued or recognised as legal tender, or where the period of recognition of the face value or physical of the currency note or coin has lapsed or expired such that it is no longer legal tender.
  7. Bills and notes payable to bearer on demand
    1. No person, except with the permission of the Authority, shall:
      1. draw, accept, make or issue any bill of exchange, promissory note or engagement for the payment of money payable to bearer on demand; or
      2. borrow, owe or take up any sum or sums of money on bills or notes payable to bearer on demand.
    2. Cheques or drafts payable to bearer on demand may be drawn on bankers or agents by their customers or constituents in respect of moneys in the hands of these bankers or agents held by them at the disposal of the persons drawing such cheques or drafts.
  8. Exchange of currency notes and coins
    1. The Authority shall arrange for the reissue and exchange of currency notes and coins subject to such conditions as may be prescribed.
    2. The Authority may, in its discretion, charge any person or class of persons such fee as may be prescribed for the reissue and exchange of currency notes or coins with the Authority under this section.
  9. Form and design of currency notes and coins
    1. The form and design of all currency notes and coins remains at the discretion of the Commissioner and must fulfil the minimum requirement that all denominations are clearly visible and easily understood.
  10. Demonetisation of currency notes and coins
    1. The Authority may withdraw any particular issue or denomination of currency notes and coins issued by the Authority which shall upon such withdrawal cease to be legal tender.
    2. Any such notification shall give holders of the currency notes and coins to be withdrawn a reasonable period, in any event not less than 6 months, within which such notes or coins shall be exchanged at their face value for other legal tender issued by the Authority.
  11. Mutilated currency notes and coins
    1. No person shall be entitled to recover from the Authority the value of any mutilated currency note or coin or any note or coin which has been illegally dealt with.
    2. The circumstances in which, and the conditions and limitations subject to which the value of mutilated currency notes or coins, or notes or coins which have been illegally dealt with, may be refunded as an act of grace shall be in the absolute discretion of the Authority.
    3. Mutilated currency notes or coins, or currency notes or coins that have been illegally dealt with, shall be repossessed by any officer of the Authority or other person authorised by the Authority when tendered to the Authority or such person, as the case may be.
  12. Restrictions on use
    1. Except with the written permission of the Commissioner, no person shall:
      1. in any size, scale or colour, use any photograph of or any drawing or design resembling any currency note or coin or part thereof, in any advertisement; or
      2. import, manufacture, sell, circulate, or otherwise distribute any merchandise or product containing such a photograph, drawing or design in any size, scale or colour.
    2. Except with the written permission of the Commissioner, no person shall import, manufacture, sell, circulate, or otherwise distribute any merchandise or product containing any design or part thereof used in or on any currency note or coin.
    3. The Commissioner may, in granting any permission under subsection (1) or (2), impose such conditions as he may deem necessary.
