  1. Short title and commencement
    1. This Act may be cited as the "Immigration Act".
    2. The following provisions of this Act are effective as of 14 May 2021.
  2. Director of Immigration
    1. The Director of Immigration, hereafter “Director”, shall be the head of the national subordinate public security department responsible for immigration and border security, and shall be appointed by the Deputy Director-General for Homeland Security in accordance with section 6 of the Public Security (Organisation) Act (Cap. 30).
    2. The powers and discretions vested in the Director under this Act, and the duties required to be discharged by him may be exercised and discharged by immigration officers duly authorised by the Director to act on his behalf.
    3. The Deputy Director-General for Homeland Security may from time to time give the Director directions of a general character, and not inconsistent with the provisions of this Act, as to the exercise of the powers and discretions conferred on the Director by, and the duties required to be discharged by the Director under, this Act or any other written law in force, to which the Director shall give full effect to such directions.
  3. Points of entry and departure
    1. The Director shall prescribe approved routes and declare such points of entry and departure as he may consider to be necessary for the purposes of this Act to be authorised immigration control posts, on a permanent basis or temporary basis, and for the classes of persons authorised to use such points.
    2. No person shall, unless compelled by accident or other reasonable cause, enter or leave, or attempt to enter or leave Imperial Royal Union except at an authorised immigration control point.
    3. Any person who contravenes this section of this Act shall be guilty of an offence.
    4. The Director shall make the necessary arrangements for ensuring adequate and trained staff to provide immigration clearance to all persons using the points of entry and departure, and in doing so shall appoint only public security officers to act as the authorised immigration officers with the lawful authority to allow or deny entry into or departure from Imperial Royal Union.
  4. Production of passports upon entry or departure
    1. Every person passing through a point of entry or departure shall produce their passport for inspection by the immigration officer, and any supplementary travel document or permit if the passport alone is unable to provide for sufficient grounds for entry or departure, or any legal document authorised by the Director in place of or in conjunction with the aforementioned documents.
  5. Immigration control points
    1. The Director shall specify an area and the corresponding limits and perimeter in which all immigration activities shall be conducted within the immigration control point.
    2. The Director shall ensure that the facilities and resources present at immigration control points allow for the proper, secure, and efficient functioning of the immigration control point.
    3. Any person, with the exception of immigration officers, who enters or attempts to enter an immigration control point, without having in his possession a passport at the time of entry, or without the intention of physically leaving or entering Imperial Royal Union, shall be guilty of an offence and shall be liable on conviction to a fine of not less than IRE 5,000.

    1. Every person, with the exception of citizens and permanent residents of Imperial Royal Union, shall be required to complete an Electronic Travel Authority, subject to the approval of an immigration officer, prior to their entry into Imperial Royal Union, in which the Director may impose an administrative fee to cover the costs of such approval.
    2. The Director may provide for facilities and staff within the immigration control point for the completion and expedited approval of the Electronic Travel Authority, in which the Director shall impose an administrative fee, which shall be higher than that of the administrative fee for approval done on a normal basis, if any at all.
    3. The Director shall determine the grounds for refusal of approval and for appeals on the Electronic Travel Authority, to which an immigration officer shall enforce. Where approval is refused on an Electronic Travel Authority, the immigration officer at the immigration control point shall refuse entry to such a person, regardless of whether the person has in his possession the valid travel documents.
    4. Where the immigration officer discovers or has sufficient grounds to believe that a false declaration has been made on an Electronic Travel Authority application, the immigration officer shall not approve the Electronic Travel Authority and may, at his discretion, refer the case for further investigation.
    5. The Director may from time to time make exemptions from the requirement of the Electronic Travel Authority for a specific class of persons, on a permanent or temporary basis, for the purposes of preventing inefficiencies arising due to administrative backlogs of Electronic Travel Authority applications by persons who may enter into Imperial Royal Union at a high frequency, or where it is expedient to do so in consideration of foreign policy objectives.
  7. Permissions for re-entry of permanent residents
    1. Any person who is a permanent resident of Imperial Royal Union shall produce their Re-Entry Permit to the immigration officer upon entry.
    2. A Re-Entry Permit shall be valid for a duration of 2 years and shall be obtained and subsequently renewed via application to an authorised officer at the National Migration Agency at least 3 months before expiry.
    3. Such an authorised officer shall be satisfied that the conditions for the grant of a Re-Entry Permit have been satisfied, in accordance with written law in force with relation to migration and residency policy, before proceeding with the issuance of the Re-Entry Permit.
  8. Right of entry
    1. Any person who is not a citizen of Imperial Royal Union may be refused entry at the discretion of the immigration officer, notwithstanding any valid documents that may be produced by the person at the immigration control point, if the immigration officer:
      1. is unable to ascertain that the conditions of entry have been satisfied based on the information furnished at the request of the immigration officer;
      2. believes or is made to believe that the person has made a false declaration or has lied in the course of interaction with the immigration officer or has not been completely honest in relation to the person's intention on entering Imperial Royal Union;
      3. discovers that the person had previously committed an immigration-related offence or overstayed his period of stay in Imperial Royal Union without reasonable grounds;
      4. suspects that the person is a fugitive or is likely to pose a public security risk; or
      5. discovers that the travel documents produced have been obtained unlawfully.
    2. Where entry is denied, the immigration officer may, as an authorised public security officer, take such person into custody and refer such person for further investigation before deportation.
    3. Any person who is a citizen of Imperial Royal Union shall not be denied their right of entry upon production of their passport, or under extraordinary circumstances as may be defined by the Director, any identity document containing a photograph of the person issued within Imperial Royal Union as proof of citizenship.
  9. Period of stay
    1. Any person who is a citizen or permanent resident of Imperial Royal Union may stay within Imperial Royal Union indefinitely.
    2. Any person who is a citizen or permanent resident of Imperial Royal Union may stay within Imperial Royal Union indefinitely.
    3. The Director shall provide for facilities within Imperial Royal Union for such persons to apply to extend their stay within Imperial Royal Union, which shall be considered on a case-by-case basis, in which the Director shall impose an administrative fee for the maintenance of such facilities and service.
    4. The Director shall determine or give effect to provisions extending the period of stay for certain classes of persons.
  10. Visas
    1. Any person who is not a citizen or permanent resident of Imperial Royal Union shall apply for a visa at any diplomatic mission of Imperial Royal Union should the intention of entry into Imperial Royal Union be for the purposes of work, education, health, and any other reasons outside of travel or if the period of stay is in excess of the stipulated period of stay in force.
    2. A visa shall be valid only for the duration as is reasonable for the conduct of activities approved by an authorised officer of the National Migration Agency in consideration of the conditions of approval of a visa and seconded by the superior consular officer in the diplomatic mission.
    3. An expired visa may be renewed within Imperial Royal Union and shall be considered on a case-by-case basis, during which the person shall be allowed a period of stay of 30 days upon the expiry of the visa and shall be barred from engaging in activities that was previously allowed under the visa during the period of 30 days or for the period between expiry and approval of renewal.
    4. The immigration officer may refuse the approval or renewal of a visa if the conditions for the grant of a visa as may be determined by the Director have not been satisfied.
    5. The immigration officer at the immigration control point may, subject to the approval of his appropriate authority, cancel and revoke any valid visa should the immigration officer have sufficient grounds to exercise his powers to refuse entry to any person under section 8 of this Act.
    6. A visa that is in effect is not a pass or other permission for, and does not confer any right on, the holder to enter Imperial Royal Union.
    7. Any person in possession of a valid visa shall apply for a Re-Entry Permit in Imperial Royal Union at least 1 month in advance should the person wish to undertake any travel out of Imperial Royal Union and return before the visa expires, in order to cover a one-off re-entry into Imperial Royal Union.
  11. Unlawful stay
    1. Upon the cancellation, revocation or expiry of any visa, permit, pass or legal document previously approved or granted by the Director, a person relying on such visa, permit, pass or legal document to stay within Imperial Royal Union shall not stay within Imperial Royal Union for more than 30 days upon such cancellation, revocation or expiry, in which no extension shall be granted, failing which, the person shall be guilty of an offence and shall be liable on conviction to a fine not exceeding IRE 5,000 or to imprisonment for a term not exceeding 6 months or to both.
  12. Procedure on arrival and departure
    1. Every vessel, aircraft, train, or vehicle arriving from or departing to a location outside of Imperial Royal Union shall berth or park at a designated immigration anchorage, airport or checkpoint, which shall be the responsibility of the master, captain or driver, failing which, they shall be guilty of an offence and shall be liable on conviction to a fine not exceeding IRE 10,000 or to imprisonment for a term not exceeding 12 months or to both.
    2. Every vessel berthed at a designated immigration anchorage shall be subject to inspection and examination and shall not be approached by or disembark any other persons except for:
      1. the master and pilot of the vessel;
      2. any government officer boarding the vessel on official duty;
      3. the owner, charterer or agent of the vessel; or
      4. a consular officer or an accredited representative of the country to which the vessel belongs.
    3. The embarkation and disembarkation of any vessel, aircraft, train or passenger transport vehicle arriving from or departing to a location outside of Imperial Royal Union shall only proceed with the permission of the immigration officer and upon satisfying the conditions and prcedures set by the Director.
    4. Every vessel, aircraft, train or passenger transport vehicle arriving from or departing to a location outside of Imperial Royal Union shall provide a passenger and crew manifest to the authorised immigration officer at least 24 hours prior to arrival or departure, in which the Director may impose an administrative fee for changes made to the manifest within the 24 hours prior to arrival or departure.
    5. The Director may prescribe additional procedures to accommodate other security functions such as customs inspections and quarantine measures, as well as any administrative fees that are to be imposed as part of this procedure and the additional procedures, if any.
  13. Mandatory disclosure by travellers
    1. Any person who arrives in Imperial Royal Union or who is about to leave Imperial Royal Union shall fully and truthfully:
      1. answer all questions and enquiries put to him by an immigration officer or a public security officer tending, directly or indirectly, to establish his identity, nationality or occupation or bearing on any of the restrictions contained in this Act or the regulations or any absolute or conditional liability on his part to any military, naval or air force service under any state or country; and
      2. disclose and produce to any such officer on demand all documents in his possession relating to those matters.
    2. All such answers and documents shall be admissible in evidence in any proceedings the person making, disclosing or producing the same.
    3. A person shall be guilty of an offence and shall be liable on conviction to a fine not exceeding IRE 2,000 or to imprisonment for a term not exceeding 6 months or to both if he:
      1. refuses to answer any question or enquiry put to him under this section;
      2. knowingly gives any false or misleading answer to any such question or enquiry;
      3. refuses or fails to produce any document in his possession when required to do so under this section;
      4. knowingly produces any false or misleading document.
  14. Medical examination
    1. The immigration officer may subject any person at an immigration control point to submit to a medical examination by a registered medical practitioner, as part of the conditions of entry should the officer deem necessary, and the results of such examination shall be forwarded to the Director
  15. Prohibited immigrant
    1. Any person who did not enter Imperial Royal Union at a prescribed point of entry via the immigration control point, or who has overstayed their period of stay, or upon interrogation found not to have satisfied the conditions of entry and area guilty of an immigration offence, shall be classified as a “prohibited immigrant”.
  16. Removal of prohibited immigrants
    1. A prohibited immigrant shall be detained at any immigration detention facility by any public security officer upon the discovery of a person of prohibited immigrant status.
    2. A prohibited immigrant shall be liable to be removed from Imperial Royal Union to his place of embarkation or to the country of his birth or citizenship or to any other port or place designated by the Director.
    3. The Director may, at any time, by order in writing, direct any of the following persons to remove, within such period as may be specified in the order, a prohibited immigrant who has arrived in Imperial Royal Union, who may be:
      1. the master of the vessel, aircraft or train which first brought the prohibited immigrant to Imperial Royal Union; or
      2. the master of any other vessel, aircraft or train belonging to the same owner or chartered by the same charterer of the vessel, aircraft or train in the aforementioned.
    4. The Director may take such action or use such force as may be necessary to ensure that an order under this section is complied with.
  17. Return after removal
    1. All prohibited immigrants removed from Imperial Royal Union shall not return to Imperial Royal Union unless a written order has been obtained from the Director and that:
      1. all proceedings against the removed person have been concluded, and that the sentences for any immigration and criminal offences have been served;
      2. a period of at least 5 years have passed since the removal from Imperial Royal Union; and
      3. the conditions of entry have been satisfied and all measures to ensure lawful entry have been undertaken.
  18. Exclusion from judicial review
    1. Any action undertaken by an immigration officer under orders or at the direction of the Director shall be excluded from judicial review except in regard to any question relating to compliance with any procedural requirement of this Act.
