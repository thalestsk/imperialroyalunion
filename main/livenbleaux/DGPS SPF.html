The Border Protection Service is a homeland security service subordinated to the Directorate-General of Public Security responsible for the protection of national borders, passport and identity services and immigration enforcement duties.


About Us

The Border Protection Service is split into 3 main departments - the Immigration Systems Department, the Border Control Department and the Immigration Enforcement Department.

Director's Office

The Director's Office manages the Border Protection Service and serves as a coordinating body for intragovernmental joint operations.

Immigration Systems Department

The Immigration Systems Department is responsible for the documentation and registration of persons within Imperial Royal Union through the National Identity Blockchain. In doing so, it also issues passports and travel documents, and communicates with the National Migration Agency and the Citizenship & Naturalisation Agency in updating residency and citizenship statuses.

Border Control Department

The Border Control Department is responsible for the day-to-day policing of the border, from the verification of travel documents to surveillance and patrolling of border areas. As part of the pre-arrival screening process, the Department also oversees the implementation of the Electronic Travel Authority for travellers prior to their arrival. To facilitate the management of the checkpoints and border areas, the Department also has holding facilities for the detention of aliens.

Immigration Enforcement Department

The Immigration Enforcement Department is responsible for all investigations pertaining to immigration-related offences, and conducts operations within Imperial Royal Union to remove suspected offenders. In dealing with courts of law on matters of immigration, it provides evidence for prosecutors in the National Justice Administration to swiftly and definitively remove and deport overstayers and offenders, and serves as the final line of defence against illegal immigration.
