Directorate-General of Public Security

The Directorate-General of Public Security is a privy council executive organisation subordinated to the National Security Council responsible for all levels of civilian law enforcement within Imperial Royal Union. From local police forces, police special forces and national criminal investigations, it also oversees the law enforcement-related security services such as the customs, prisons and border forces. Duties pertaining to espionage, surveillance and intelligence-related activities fall under the remit of the Directorate-General of State Security instead.


About Us

The Director-General and Deputy Directors-General of the Directorate-General are all promoted from within the senior leadership itself at the discretion of the Emperor. Within the Directorate-General, command authority does not regularly rest with the senior leadership, which serves policy, administrative and strategic planning roles and seldom interfere with field operations by the departments subordinated to the Directorate-General.

Organisation

Law enforcement is managed at 3 levels in Imperial Royal Union - National, Territorial and Cantonal. Day-to-day policing and criminal investigation is conducted by the Cantonal Police Departments, supported by the Territorial Police Services which provide coverage for areas not within the feasible reach of the Cantonal Police Departments. At the national level, the Homeland Security Agencies and Services generally deal with international and inter-territorial crime, as well as border protection, the prisons and customs.

Each Territory is assigned a Public Security Council, which plans for the operational requirements of the Territorial Police Service and the Cantonal Police Services within the Territory. Representatives of the Cantonal Governments and the Territorial Administrative Authorities form part of their respective Public Security Councils with the remainder filled by senior officers appointed by the Directorate-General's leadership.

Underlying Legislation

Law enforcement officers within the Directorate-General are known as public security officers. Public security officers derive their powers and are governed by the relevant statutes under the following Acts. In addition, the Cantonal Governments may enact further legislation to enhance the powers of public security officers.

Cap. 30 Public Security (Organisation) Act
Cap. 31 Public Security (Powers) Act
Cap. 32 Public Security (Conduct) Act
