Main Article: Directorate-General of Public Security

The Cyberspace Security Agency is a homeland security agency subordinated to the Directorate-General of Public Security responsible for preventing and investigating computer crimes and crimes committed via the Internet.


About Us

The Cyberspace Security Agency is split into 3 operational departments - Cybersecurity Policy, Cybercrime, and Internet Fraud.

Director's Office

The Director's Office manages the Cyberspace Security Agency and serves as a liaison body for intragovernmental affairs.

    • Deputy Director, Management | SAC Joanna Avery (Ms.)

Cybersecurity Policy Department

The Cybersecurity Policy Department determines and directs the cybersecurity policy across the whole of HIM Government and provides cybersecurity advice and software to the Territorial Administrative Authorities and Cantonal Governments. It also administers cybersecurity audits to improve the overall security and integrity of government computer and information systems.

  • Senior Assistant Director, Cybersecurity Policy | AC Aldo Hunter (Mr.)
    • Assistant Director, Systems Architecture | DAC Hans Oettinger (Mr.)
    • Assistant Director, Cybersecurity Audit | DAC Clive Eldrich (Mr.)

Cybercrime Department

The Cybercrime Department investigates criminal activity committed on the Internet and with computers, primarily dealing with malware and ransomware attacks, and also conduct the general surveillance of the Internet and the dark web.

  • Senior Assistant Director, Cybercrime | AC Gavyn Campbell (Mr.)
    • Assistant Director, Dark Web Surveillance | DAC Tatiana Reznikova (Ms.)
    • Assistant Director, Malware Intelligence | DAC Wilson Delasalle (Mr.)
    • Assistant Director, Diagnosis & Intervention | DAC Erik Grabol (Mr.)

Internet Fraud Department

The Internet Fraud Department investigates all instances of fraud committed via computerised means, such as digital forgery, phishing attacks and online scams. It works closely with the National Investigation Agency to combat transnational fraud.
