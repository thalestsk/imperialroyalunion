The National Investigation Agency is a homeland security agency subordinated to the Directorate-General of Public Security responsible for criminal investigations at the national and international level, and is Imperial Royal Union's highest crime-fighting agency. The Agency is commonly abbreviated as AGNES after the French name of the Agency.


About Us

The National Investigation Agency is split into 4 divisions - National Security, Operations Support, Criminal Intelligence, and Criminal Investigation.

Office of the Director

The Office of the Director manages the Agency and oversees the crime-fighting operations and priorities.

National Security Division

The National Security Division collects and analyses intelligence pertaining to imminent threats to national security, and conducts counter-terrorism and counter-intelligence operations domestically. It works closely with the Department of Internal Security & Counterespionage from the Directorate-General of State Security to maintain national security.

Operations Support Division

The Operations Support Division is responsible for providing support to field operations and investigations of the Agency, from forensics to tactical officers, as well as technical and database support. Rapid Incident Response Contingents are maintained in each Canton by the Agency to complement local law enforcement, while the Tactical Intervention Force fulfils the role of the special operations force of the Agency.

Criminal Intelligence Division

The Criminal Intelligence Division is responsible for all intelligence-gathering missions within the Agency, setup specifically outside of the Criminal Investigation Division to ensure the independence and admissibility of recorded material and to reduce bias during the investigation process. It works closely with the National Surveillance Institute of the Directorate-General of State Security in running its surveillance operations.

Criminal Investigation Division

The Criminal Investigation Division is the largest division within the Agency and fulfils the main responsibility of criminal investigations. It conducts investigation for cases beyond the jurisdictional scope or means of Cantonal Police Agencies, as well as transnational crime, where it works closely with other homeland security agencies such as the Border Protection Service on matters of high-level trafficking.
