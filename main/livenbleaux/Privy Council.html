Privy Council of Livenbleaux

The Privy Council of Livenbleaux is a council appointed at the sole discretion of the Emperor. Sometimes referred to as the "Inner Cabinet", it meets to discuss matters of the state at the highest level before delegating responsibilities to the subordinate levels of HIM Government. The Privy Council is responsible for the core functions of HIM Government, namely defence, security, foreign affairs and supervision, as well as the coordination of the 3 branches of government. The 3 branches are unified under the Emperor; there is no pretense of separation of powers. Apart from the Emperor as the chairman of the Privy Council, there are 5 other members of the Privy Council.

Privy Council Scope

There are 4 Privy Council Sub-Councils - the Council of War, the Council of Justice, the National Security Council, and the Sovereign Affairs Council, covering defence, justice, security and foreign affairs respectively. The Council of War and the Council of Justice are directly headed by the Emperor as the chairman, while the Lord High Constable chairs the National Security Council and the Lord High Minister chairs the Sovereign Affairs Council. There are 6 Privy Council Executive Organisations and 3 Organs of State directly reporting to the Privy Council and its Sub-Councils. The Lord High Consul administers and manages the Public Service Secretariat (with the assistance of the Permanent Secretaries) while the Lord High President oversees the Estates-General.
