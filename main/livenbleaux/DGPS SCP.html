The Correctional Reform Service is a homeland security service subordinated to the Directorate-General of Public Security responsible for administration and operation of correctional and rehabilitative services.


About Us

The Correctional Reform Service is split into 1 main departments and 5 separate prison systems. Under such a system, each Territory operates its own prison system, which can then be tailored to the rehabilitative posture of each Canton within the Territory. Transport of prisoners and offenders awaiting trial depends on the arrangement between the investigative authorities, the courts and the prison systems.

Director's Office

The Director's Office manages the Correctional Reform Service and serves as a coordinating body for the Service, while handling the administration of the Service.

Policy Department

The Policy Department is responsible for formulating and administering rehabilitative policy, correctional conduct policy, as well as advising on prisoner rights within the 5 prison systems. It also handles most of the liaison between the other homeland security agencies and services for instances of prisoner transfer and production interviews for investigations.

Islandus Penitentiary System

The Islandus Penitentiary System is 1 of the 4 territorial prison systems, comprising of all prisons within the Territory of Islandus. Due to the geographical location of the Port Imperial Capital Territory, prisons there fall within the jurisdiction of the Islandus Penitentiary System. The Islandus Penitentiary System has a greater focus on rehabilitation, with the greatest proportion of spending allocated to rehabilitation programmes.

Imperialia Corrections System

The Imperialia Corrections System is 1 of the 4 territorial prison systems providing coverage for the Territory of Imperialia. As part of security measures and owing to large swaths of land, most prisons under the Imperialia Corrections System are located close to existing security facilities, such as military bases or public security training areas. The Imperialia Corrections System has consistently ranked as the most secure prison system and houses the largest proportion of dangerous criminals.

Eastern Territory Reformative Training System

The Eastern Territory Reformative Training System is 1 of the 4 territorial prison systems, covering the Eastern Territory. As part of the rehabilitative regime, there is a greater focus on reform, rehabilitation and re-education, and as a result, produces the lowest proportion of re-offenders. The Eastern Territory Reformative Training System also boasts record operating profit through prison production. At sentencing, criminals with high propensity for reform may opt to be sent to the Eastern Territory Reformative Training System instead of their respective territorial prison system.

Southern Isles Prison System

The Southern Isles Prison System is 1 of the 4 territorial prison systems responsible for the Southern Isles Territory. It has a notorious reputation for the worst living conditions of the other territorial prison systems, although most criminals incarcerated within the Southern Isles Prison System have a higher likelihood of remaining within the prisons for the remainder of their lives. Only 1 in 20 prisoners are ever released from the Southern Isles Prison System.

Special Detention Facility

The Special Detention Facility is a shared detention facility between the Directorate-General of Public Security and the Directorate-General of State Security managed by the Service. Unlike the other prison systems, the Facility accepts detainees detained without trial and select prisoners not sentenced to capital punishment. Correction officers must serve for at least 5 years before being eligible to serve at the Facility. The Facility operates multiple detention centres as well as vocational re-education institutions.
