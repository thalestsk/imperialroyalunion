Public Service Secretariat

The Public Service Secretariat is responsible for the administration of the civil service. It is the employer of all public servants on behalf of HIM Government. The importance of the Secretariat is reflected in the Privy Council of Livenbleaux, where the Secretariat is headed by the Lord High Consul with the assistance of the Chief Permanent Secretary and the Permanent Secretaries. The Public Service Secretariat performs executive functions in support of the core functions of HIM Government held by the Privy Council while providing oversight, guidance, regulatory and advisory functions to the Cantonal Governments and Territorial Administrative Authorities. Executive power not delegated by the Emperor to the Privy Council Executive Organisations or by an Act of Estates to Statutory Departments are automatically assumed by the Public Service Secretariat, which may then be delegated to Government Commissions or Executive Support Offices.


The Permanent Secretaries

There are a total of 8 Permanent Secretaries reporting to the Chief Permanent Secretary. The appointment of all public service officers to senior positions in HIM Government is decided between the Chief Permanent Secretary and the Permanent Secretaries.

Cabinet Offices

Each Permanent Secretary heads their own Secretariat Group, which comprises of its own Cabinet Office (Westminster systems refer to these as the "Private Offices"), out of which the senior management within the Secretariat Group operate from. The Lord Consul and the Chief Permanent Secretary perform coordinating, supervisory, and advisory functions within all Secretariat Groups. Permanent Secretaries use their respective Cabinet Offices to provide direction and guidance to the departments that report directly to them.
