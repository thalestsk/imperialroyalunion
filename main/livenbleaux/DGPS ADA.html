Main Article: Directorate-General of Public Security

The Customs & Excise Agency is a homeland security agency subordinated to the Directorate-General of Public Security responsible for enforcing the import and export laws of Imperial Royal Union at the border checkpoints, with priorities in combating cross-border smuggling and trafficking of prohibited and restricted goods.


About Us

The Customs & Excise Agency is split into 3 operational departments - Checkpoint Operations, Policy & Regulation, and Investigation.

Director's Office

The Director's Office manages the Customs & Excise Agency and serves as a liaison body for intragovernmental affairs and joint operations.

Checkpoint Operations Department

The Checkpoint Operations Department is responsible for checks and inpections on all inbound and outbound goods passing through border checkpoints, with main objective of intercepting prohibited and restricted goods and deterring against smuggling and trafficking.

Policy & Regulation Department

The Policy & Regulation Department oversees the overall customs policy of Imperial Royal Union, working closely with the Public Revenue Administration to determine the classification of goods under the harmonised indices, as well as developing the Restricted Goods Schedule and the national biosecurity regime against imported agricultural and livestock products. It also conducts licensing and regulatory checks on entities selling and distributing duty-exempt goods.

Investigation Department

The Investigation Department is responsible for investigating violations of customs and excise laws, often with the goal of recovering revenue lost from duty evasion along with the imposition of economic penalties.
