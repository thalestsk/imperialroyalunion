The Estates-General

The Estates-General is the legislature of Imperial Royal Union. While the Emperor is the source of legislative authority, the Emperor recognises the importance of the consultative role of the Estates-General, hence all legislature is originated and instituted as Acts of Estates. The bicameral Estates-General comprises of two houses - the appointed Senate (upper house) and elected Chamber of Deputies (lower house). The Chamber of Deputies is a consultative forum for national matters and has limited powers with regards to local legislatures, while the Senate is granted powers to pass and repeal bills and acts on national matters and affairs of local legislatures.
