Chief Presiding Officer

The Chief Presiding Officer of the Chamber of Deputies is in charge of facilitating the discussions that go on in the chamber itself. The Chief Presiding Officer is the highest-ranking member of the Chamber of Deputies, although he is outranked by the Ranking Members of the Senate and Senators from the upper house of the Estates-General. Apart from deciding on the order of business within the Chamber, the Chief Presiding Officer is also responsible for drafting the Standing Orders of the Chamber of Deputies and ensuring that Chamber rules are adhered to and enforced. The appointment of the Chief Presiding Officer is granted at the sole discretion of the Emperor to a non-elected official outside of the Estates-General.

By tradition, the Chief Presiding Officer usually appoints the Chairmen of political blocs as Deputy Presiding Officers and Secretary-Generals of political blocs as Leaders of the Chamber.
