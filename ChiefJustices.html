Supreme Court of Judicature Chief Justices

The Chief Justices of Imperial Royal Union

The highest-ranking judicial officer is designated as a Chief Justice. Between the 7 Chief Justices, the Emperor appoints one of them as the Lord High Justice, an appointment allowing him to act as the honorary head of the judiciary, primus inter pares. All 7 Chief Justices are appointed at the Emperor's discretion, and all 7 Chief Justices are considered the ranking members of the Supreme Court of Judicature. Chief Justices are appointed for life until they step down at the customary although non-mandatory age of 80. The youngest Chief Justice to be appointed is Sandey Lexmann, appointed at the age of 58. The appointments of all subordinate justices, clerks and registrars of the courts are managed by the 7 Chief Justices as ranking members. Appeals to the National Appellate Court and "constitutional" matters are heard by at least 1 Chief Justice alongside 2 Justices of Appeals, while selected revisionary authority on cases heard in the subordinate courts are reviewed by the Lord High Justice and 2 Chief Justices.
