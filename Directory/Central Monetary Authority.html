About

The Central Monetary Authority is the central bank and central financial services regulator of Imperial Royal Union. As the Imperial Royale Elite, Imperial Royal Union's legal tender, operates on a floating exchange rate system, the Authority's key responsibility is in ensuring that the float is within a reasonable range based on an undisclosed basket of goods, commodities, financial instruments and other assets. Its regulatory duties are complementary to its functions as the central bank. It is also responsible for the storage of the national reserves.


Leadership

The Authority is headed by the Treasurer-General. The Treasurer-General is in turn assisted by the Deputy Treasurer-General and Assistant Treasurer-Generals. The Treasurer-General is nominated by the Chief Permanent Secretary of the Public Service Secretariat at the recommendation of the Permanent Secretary for Economic Affairs and appointed by the Emperor.

Central Reserve Bank Board

Central banking responsibilities are managed by the Central Reserve Bank Board, consisting of the Treasurer-General as its chairman, the Treasury Directors nominated by the Chief Permanent Secretary at the recommendation of the Permanent Secretary for Economic Affairs and the Treasurer-General and appointed by the Emperor, and the Governors of the Territorial Reserve Banks, appointed by the Lord Administrators of the constituent Territories of Imperial Royal Union.
