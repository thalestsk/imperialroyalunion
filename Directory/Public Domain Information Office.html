About

The Public Domain Information Office is the national agency responsible for fighting the spread of fakes news and misinformation. Alongside with the Government Media & Information Office, the Office has the authority to issue correction orders against newspapers, websites, blogs and any form of news media, be it through the web or through traditional channels. The Office also relies on private citizens to lodge complaints about fake news sources, and may even take down websites or traditional media deemed to be persistent offenders. In such cases, follow-up action may be referred to the National Justice Administration for civil suits or the Directorate-General of Public Security should there be any criminal intent.


Leadership

The Public Domain Information Office is headed by the Assistant Permanent Secretary (Education), who is appointed directly by the Permanent Secretary for Education.
