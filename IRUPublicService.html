The Public Service Secretariat

The Public Service Secretariat is responsible for the administration of the civil service. It is the employer of all public servants on behalf of HIM Government. As part of the executive branch of government, subordinate departments under the direct purview of the Secretariat are referred to as "Statutory Departments" since such departments operate by statute and only receive executive instructions from the leadership of HIM Government occasionally. The importance of the Secretariat is reflected in the Privy Council of Livenbleaux, where the Secretariat is headed by the Lord High Consul with the assistance of the Chief Permanent Secretary and the Permanent Secretaries. The Secretariat is also responsible for the Government Media & Information Office, the government's centralised inter-department press office.


While the scope of the Secretariat with regards to foreign policy, defence and national security is limited, the Secretariat retains responsibility for the administration of auxiliary departments that support the overall umbrella organisations under the direct authority of the Council of War, National Security Council and Sovereign Affairs Council.
