The Territorial Police Units

There are 4 territorial police units in Imperial Royal Union, responsible for their own respective Territories. Jurisdictional authority is limited to the respective Territories and where cantonal police units have not been notified or are not available to assume responsibility. It is also responsible for cross-cantonal enforcement and coordination between cantonal police units. It is primarily responsible for providing police presence in rural areas where cantonal police units may have difficulty reaching. The territorial police units are not superior to the cantonal police units but a supporting force for holistic law enforcement. Each territorial police unit is headed by a Superintendent-General, who reports to both the Directorate-General of Public Security and their respective Territorial Administrative Authority.


Of the 4 territorial police units, the Southern Isles Maritime Gendarmerie stands out from the rest due to the geographical features of the Southern Isles. Since most of the developed areas in the Southern Isles are already well-covered by the cantonal police units, the Southern Isles Maritime Gendarmerie is more focused on coast guard duties throughout the Territory as well as enforcement duties at the multiple marine conservation areas or sanctuaries and the thousands of outlying uninhabited islands of the Territory, often cooperating with the coast guard and maritime security forces of the Imperialean National Armed Forces' Universal Defence Command.
