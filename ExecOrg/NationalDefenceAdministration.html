The National Defence Administration

The National Defence Administration is the civilian arm of the Imperialean National Armed Forces which support the administration of the armed forces. It is the equivalent of the Ministry of Defence of other nations, although a major difference is that the Administration does not exercise any form of command authority. Apart from its primary administrative role, it also plays a secondary role in the planning and development of the armed forces for the future. The Administration is split into 4 departments, each reporting to the Director-General of Defence Administration.

Defence Management Group

The Defence Management Group runs the financial and professional management services of the Administration. It relies on the Joint Staff to consolidate on financial affairs and professional requirements before assisting to fulfill requests while exercising oversight on finances.

Plans & Transformation Directorate

The Plans & Transformation Directorate is responsible for the future development of the armed forces. It is a reviewing authority tasked to renew and refine systems and processes within the military, as well as develop new state-of-the-art systems and technology for combat deployment.

Department of National Service

The Department of National Service is responsible for administering the conscription service as part of the Enlistment Act (Cap. 4), as well as matters pertaining to reserve forces and retired forces, including the pension fund and post-service benefits and services for veterans.

Krigsmaskin

The Krigsmaskin (Swedish for "War Machine") is the planning office and support group for the Chief of War Staff of the Imperialean National Armed Forces. At the outbreak of war, the Krigsmaskin is responsible for transforming the nation to a state of war and steering the war economy. It is headed by the Chief of War Staff and the only department within the Administration to be staffed by both military and civilian personnel.
