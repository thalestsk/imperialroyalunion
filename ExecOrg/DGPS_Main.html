The Directorate-General of Public Security

The Directorate-General of Public Security is an executive organisation of HIM Government responsible for law enforcement and public order in Imperial Royal Union. It is headed by the Director-General of Public Security and comprises of the national police units, territorial police units and cantonal police units, which engage in public order duties at a different level of administrative divisions. The Director-General of Public Security reports to the National Security Council, which is represented by the Lord Constable in the Privy Council of Livenbleaux.


Organisational Structure

The Directorate-General is split into 3 major departmental groups - the Homeland Security Group, Frontline Police Group and Administration Group. The Homeland Security Group comprises of the national police units, while the Frontline Police Group comprises of the territorial and cantonal police units. The Administration Group is responsible for the overall administrative requirements of the Directorate-General at large, and is primarily focused on policy work and consolidation of reports, accounts and requisitions of the rest of the Directorate-General. Each group is headed by a Deputy Director-General.
