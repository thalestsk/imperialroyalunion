The Senate Committees

The Senate Committees are committees setup by the President of the Senate to coordinate on certain matters of national importance. Contrary to its name, the Senate Committees actually comprise of both Senators and Deputies, since cooperation between the consultative and deliberative houses are fundamental to smooth passage of the legislative agenda. There are three types of Senate Committees - Standing Committees, Special Committees and Subcommittees, although within the Senate, the Senate Committees are not responsible for introducing or specialising in certain legislation but serve to ensure that communication across the Estates-General is smooth and purposeful. Each Subcommittee may have its own Working Committee within the Chamber of Deputies, which is purely for discussion within the Chamber of Deputies and as such are authoritative in the lower house.


Standing Committees of the Senate

There are two Standing Committees of the Senate, the Standing Committee on Nominations and the Standing Committee on Privileges. The former is responsible for proposing nominees to the Emperor for vacant appointments, while the latter is responsible for procedures in the Senate and privileges of the Senators.

Special Committees of the Senate

The Special Committees of the Senate are created on an ad-hoc basis and are usually dissolved upon completion of its objectives. Currently, no Special Committees exist.


Subcommittees of the Senate

The Subcommittees of the Senate are responsible for a multitude of affairs and are open for cooperation and collaboration with their counterparts in the Chamber of Deputies through its internal Working Committees. Subcommittees are created and dissolved accordingly by the President of the Senate. Its members are appointed and may be replaced by the President, First Vice-President or the Second Vice-President.
